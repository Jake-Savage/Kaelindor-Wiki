## Overview
Nerrin is a long-abandoned mountain mining village. Seventy years ago a cave-in killed half of its seventy miners; in the aftermath, people began vanishing two or three at a time until the village was wholly deserted. The cause was a nest of [[meenlocks]] that took root from a Feywild intrusion ([[the-feywild]]) tied to a mountain tarn above the village, triggered by the emotional trauma of the disaster. Black oily moss — later identified as [[the-uthrel]] — was seen in the mine.

## Notable inhabitants & sites
- **The mayor's house** — holds [[mayor-duvesin]]'s journals, which chart her descent into madness, alongside Feywild books.
- **The offering tree** — where Lorna's locket was left.
- **Lorna's grave** — Trevick tended it and earned a blessing of [[naelos]] for the act.
- The Duvesin family bears a snowdrop signet ring ([[duvesin-signet-ring]]).

## Events here
- S1.01: The party destroys six [[meenlocks]] in the [[nerrin-mine]].
- S1.04: The party returns to confirm the moss-tunnels are gone (the nest died with the meenlocks); the mine is reopened (see [[reopen-the-nerrin-mine]]).

## DM notes
Nerrin is the campaign's first encounter with the moss that becomes [[the-uthrel]] and with a Feywild intrusion — both seeds for the central Chapter 2 threat. The snowdrop motif of the Duvesin signet later names the party's home, [[snowdrop-house]].
