## Overview
The Mirrowen — known to almost everyone as **the Tangles** — is a large region of magical disruption reached, from the party's direction, by descending south out of [[trevaryn|Trevaryn]]. It is technically counted among the countries of the League and holds a seat on the Council, but in practice it is not really a country: it is an area too damaged to belong cleanly to anyone, which the surrounding powers have peeled off into its own jurisdiction. The land is physically fragmented — deep valleys, jagged terrain — and lacks a consistent, reliable geography. Some landmarks can be relied on, but other parts shift; sometimes whole villages disappear. Seen from the heights of Trevaryn, the landscape beyond [[greywatch|Greywatch]]'s cleared patch looks irregular and "not quite right."

No one knows how the Mirrowen came to be this way, but there is strong consensus it was not always so — something happened, whether a natural event or a great act of magic, before current civilisational records. As far as anyone remembers, it has always been like this.

## What is known
The party (chiefly [[fabian-perennius]], taught about it as part of his League education, and [[trevick]], from neighbouring [[trevaryn|Trevaryn]]) recalls the following (DM-established world facts, S2.28):

- It is **borderline habitable**. People do live there, but the central and southern areas are the most disrupted; population is low. The south is effectively impassable, so goods and traffic move north toward Trevaryn.
- It is **psychically dangerous**: latent energy here drives a high incidence of people losing their minds, on top of the physical dangers.
- It throngs with **strange, monstrous creatures**. Scholars treat them broadly as aberrations; explanations differ over whether they are mutations born of planes in unnatural contact, or things from no known plane — more fundamentally alien. There is no scholarly agreement; large-scale emergences have not happened for hundreds of years.
- The underlying phenomenon is **planar fragmentation**: planes that would normally never touch butt up against one another. Planar portals to specific other planes sometimes appear spontaneously or stay briefly stable, but more often reality is simply twisted together in untidy ways.
- The land **throws up echoes and mimicry** — taking on the shape or guise of things it has touched, with more or less solidity. This is a shape the phenomena take, not a race of shapeshifters; the DM distinguished it explicitly from changelings or doppelgangers. Outside Greywatch there is a strong cultural emphasis on vouching for identity, because anyone who goes off alone is suspect on return.
- It has a **very high incidence of innate magical gifts** — many sorcerers are born here. Some come hoping their children will be born with power, or to find the rare, dangerous places where a magical spark can be "fanned" into greater power.
- It holds **valuable magical resources**: plants that grow nowhere else, and ancient sites that the shifting land occasionally uncovers, unlooted, for brief windows of exploration. Treasure-seekers come to make their fortunes; few return.
- It is considered **sacred to the [[itharis|Itharians]]** — a place of thresholds, gateways, crossings, and ambiguity. They acknowledge its blighted qualities but find it fascinating and reverent rather than merely horrible.

## Notable inhabitants & sites
- [[greywatch|Greywatch]] ("the Beacon") — the only substantial town, a walled, heavily-warded garrison town at the Mirrowen's northern edge.
- Roads connect Greywatch north to **Drake Mount** ([[trevaryn|Trevaryn]]) via the Drake's Road, and to **Mooncross** in Kalredor to the (south/west).

## Events here
- S2.28: The party arrives at the Mirrowen's edge and enters [[greywatch|Greywatch]], pursuing the [[soren-dragan]] lead (see [[the-korrathian-conspiracy]]).
- S2.29: In [[greywatch]] the party learns the region is ruled through Greywatch's elected **Warden of the Beacon** ([[ivo-pierron|Ivo Pierron]]) with the approval of [[trevaryn|Trevaryn]], and that townsfolk see "two powers" here — the [[itharis|Itharian]] Church and [[drake-mount|Drake Mount]]. Gnome gossip corroborates the region's **identity-mimicry**: travellers tell of meeting people on the road "who shouldn't have been there," and a strong culture of vouching for anyone who travels alone. The destroyed village of [[kethons-fold|Kethon's Fold]] (swallowed by a sinkhole ~10 weeks earlier) is named as the party's next destination.

## DM notes
- Canonical spelling is provisional: the DM gives the proper name as "the Mirrowen" (also heard as "Merowen"/"Meroen"; the S2.27 recap used "Meroen"). "The Tangles" is the universal colloquial name.
- The cause of the Mirrowen's planar damage is an open, pre-historical mystery. Whether it connects to [[the-horizon-doors]], the [[the-disappearance-of-dragons|disappearance of the dragons]], or the wider cosmology is unestablished — kept separate from the [[the-uthrel|uthrel]] incursion thread.
