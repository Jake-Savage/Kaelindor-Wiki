## Recap
Rell and Dakir trail Serris to a boatshed, where he and a boatwright named Jorren are strengthening a small rowboat with "storm marks" for rough weather — clearly so Serris can recreate the old Stormblind expedition and be welcomed by Rhynharran. They later examine the boat, finding it heavily warded only for staying afloat in storms, with no provision for keeping occupants alive.

At dusk, Caspian, Fabian, Mags, and Trevick observe the Quickened with the druid Anwen Ireth. They watch the otter-like, barb-tentacled Yarrows and a short-lived "driftcrown." Anwen, guarded, admits the Quickened are troubled because their natural place is "under pressure," that something has changed even if nothing changed on the town's side, and hints repeatedly that the cause lies far to the west, deep beneath the lake where larger Quickened dwell. A novice, Lillian, is troubled by what she records. Meanwhile Nessa guides Mags, Trevick, and the others deep into the forest along subtle markers, many now burnt or blackened; Trevick leaves a respectful written offering, and Mags collects west-side moss. Nessa speaks of Illyra's elven heritage and the prejudice driving her away.

## Key facts learned
- Serris and the boatwright Jorren are secretly building a storm-worthy boat so Serris can recreate the Stormblind expedition; the boat is warded only to survive storms, not to protect its occupants.
- Anwen confirms the Quickened are troubled because their place is "under pressure"; she points west, deep beneath the lake, as the source, where larger Quickened dwell.
- The Quickened react to fey-linked magic and "feed" on concentrated fey energy.
- Many forest markers are now burnt/blackened; Trevick can read them only with Nessa's help.
- Illyra has elven blood (not eastern high-elf); such heritage makes one a target in the current climate.

## Threads opened / advanced
- Serris's plan to sail into a storm is revealed as dangerous and likely fatal.
- The deeper cause is pinned to the far west of the lake/forest.
- Trevick's bond with the forest and Nessa deepens; the offering he leaves draws a response next session.
