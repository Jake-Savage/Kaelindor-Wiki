## Recap
Reaching Bridgemarch ahead of Dakir and Fabian, Mags, Rell, and Caspian disguise themselves and stake out the contact's address — an unremarkable, magically warded metalworking shop in the artisan district run by two long-resident tieflings (likely Korrath-born). Their detection magic is deflected by concealment wards. Deciding against storming it themselves, they send Chancellor Wicklow a message requesting guards and mages.

Captain Thoronby's squad — equipped against elemental magic — raids the shop; a ward-blast injures the first soldiers, and the two tieflings kill themselves with acid before capture. Mags heals the wounded. Behind the fireplace, after dispelling a potent trap, the party finds a blood-and-ash shrine to the Furnace Brood, ritually used many times, with locked chests guarded by a shadowy tentacle illusion that necrotically wounds a soldier (Mags heals him). The evidence is taken to the Palace District. In audience, Wicklow treats Korrathian cultists in the city seriously, surmises the tieflings (resident 9 years, posing as Korrath refugees) were a contact point rather than targeting the city, and renegotiates the party's pay (1400g for Greymere on a report; 400g for the new task). Mags and Caspian catch Wicklow lying when he denies knowing of the uthrel, and he takes keen interest in Kroll's marked map.

## Key facts learned
- The Bridgemarch contact was two tieflings (resident ~9 years, posing as Korrath refugees) running a warded metalworking shop; they suicided with acid rather than be taken.
- Behind the shop's fireplace is a repeatedly-used blood/ash shrine to the Furnace Brood, with trapped chests guarded by a necrotic shadow-tentacle illusion.
- Concealment magic is a hallmark of how the Korrathians operate on the Material Plane.
- Wicklow lies about not knowing the uthrel — Mags and Caspian detect he does know of it.
- Wicklow takes keen interest in Kroll's marked map and keeps it; party paid 1400g (Greymere) + 400g (new task) on delivery of a report.

## Threads opened / advanced
- The Korrathian conspiracy escalates to a city-level threat investigated with the crown.
- Wicklow's concealed knowledge of the uthrel becomes a major open suspicion.
- The marked map and the suicided cell leave the conspiracy's full purpose unresolved — the chapter's cliff-hanger.
