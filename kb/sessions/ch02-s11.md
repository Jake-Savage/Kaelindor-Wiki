## Recap
Rell identifies the over-excited man as Serris, who works at the temple of Rhynharran. Mags has Trevick (and, unknown to him, Rell) follow Serris home, where he is found praying fervently at a private shrine while his worried parents listen. At dusk, Mags casts Water Walk and goes out onto the lake to greet and make offerings to the Quickened; Fabian and Trevick delight in walking on the water.

The next morning the party visits Lord Mathis Irdane (mid-twenties, new lord) at Irdane Hall. He explains the Quickened of the lake — called the Yarrows — usually only tease fisherfolk but recently killed Bran Hosteigg and Orren Bell, overturning their boats. The trouble began around a new "safety" charm given to Bran by the hedge witch Illyra Fen, who has since vanished; the Yarrows seemed determined to tear the charm away. The Greenheart, Thalos Briarwood, counsels only patience. Outside, Mags intercepts Lieutenant Lena Ashfield, an aide to the Reeve worried about the crisis's wider economic ripples, and helps her present her report; the party agrees to visit Nessa (another hedge witch) and Rhynharran's Hall next.

## Key facts learned
- "Serris" works at the temple of Rhynharran, prays fervently at a private home shrine, and is the man whose excitement Rell flagged.
- The Yarrows (Quickened of the lake) killed Bran Hosteigg and Orren Bell; the trouble began around a "safety" charm from the hedge witch Illyra Fen, now missing.
- Illyra Fen has elven blood and made herself scarce; orders exist to detain her if she returns.
- The Greenheart Thalos Briarwood counsels patience and won't explain.
- Nessa, another hedge witch with firbolg blood, lives on Greymere's western edge and can guide people into the forest.
- The Stormblind memorial commemorates 22 followers of Rhynharran lost calming a great storm centuries ago.

## Threads opened / advanced
- The Serris mystery (his fervour, dreams, and possible heritage) opens.
- Illyra Fen and her charm become central leads to the Quickened's change.
- The forest/Eldryn and the western fire emerge as the deeper cause to pursue.
