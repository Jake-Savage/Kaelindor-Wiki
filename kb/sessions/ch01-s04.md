## Recap
Shalfay asks the party to find two students, Isabella Falcrae and Bran Oris, who absconded into the Crystal Cave three months ago seeking a way to escape arranged duties. Caspian researches the cave — created 250 years ago by the Tower mage Perfora and her elven love Cerwin as a refuge, haunted, with a frozen waterfall that grants aid to those who seek sanctuary.

Within, the party clear a deadly mud field and reach the waterfall, passing through into a beautiful feywild garden. They meet revelling satyrs led by Zanim, the unicorns Stargleam and Silverlily, and ultimately the Archfey known as the Gardener — blue-skinned, robed in greenery — who is curious about Dakir. When shown the moss, the unicorns are horrified and the Gardener disintegrates the sample, calling it a danger to the sanctuary and firmly refusing further discussion. The party solve the maze (collecting dog-rose, mistletoe, oak, and shamrock leaves from leprechauns, dryads, barkburrs, and fairies) to reach the palace.

There they find Isabella and Bran, charmed and happy, alongside two reformed thieves, Argos and Hamish, who chose to stay forever. The students write farewell letters and give the party tokens (a crested rapier, Bran's signet ring, a greater healing potion) for their families. Dakir and Caspian press the couple to question whether they are happy or merely prisoners; Bran convinces Isabella to leave, and the party escort them out — the waterfall sealing behind them. Back at the Tower, Isabella coldly burns her letter; Bran keeps his.

Over the following days Shalfay rewards the party (Caspian's portable library-hole, Trevick's necklace of adaptation, Dakir's wand of lightning bolts, a folding boat, a magical quill) and uses the prophetic books to crudely answer Caspian's moss question: a clear warning against contamination. The party return to Nerrin to find the moss-tunnels gone (the nest died with the meenlocks), then back to Stonehaven to settle loose ends, sell goods, and collect Muriel's armour.

## Key facts learned
- The Crystal Cave's feywild garden is a sanctuary built by Perfora and Cerwin; those who drink from the fountain stay and can never return once they leave.
- The Gardener (an Archfey) treats the moss as a grave danger and refuses to explain it.
- Shalfay's divination on the moss returns a "warning against contamination": the Feywild and under-dark are both hyper-fertile and fear the moss's spread; the material plane is less hospitable, so the moss's advance was caused by the Feywild's touch.
- Trevick can read elvish; Caspian learns firbolg-style naming conventions (names as descriptions).

## Threads opened / advanced
- Missing students found and returned home; quest resolved.
- The moss / contamination — confirmed extremely dangerous and feywild-linked, but its ultimate source remains unknown.
- Nerrin confirmed cleansed; mine reopened.
