## Recap
This session is largely a narrated time-skip and catch-up, with no combat.

Before leaving [[bridgemarch]], the party spends a few more days finishing inquiries into [[lord-torston|Torston]] and reporting to the Chancellor; they depart on 22 Threadbind. At a parting luncheon, [[benny-carter|Benny]] — now thriving and training with the Queen's Guard — nervously reveals that he was the one who reported the party's suspicions about Torston to the authorities: after the affair, Torston tried to draw him in further, Benny refused, and that is how Torston's conspiring came to official notice. The disclosure has earned Benny official favour. The party reassures him.

The journey to [[greywatch]] takes about five weeks: north out of Bridgemarch, hugging the Offendale–[[trevaryn|Trevaryn]] border, then east to the Drake's Road down from [[drake-mount|Drake Mount]]. Most of the time is spent levelling and honing skills. [[caspian-talon|Caspian]] and [[fabian-perennius|Fabian]] talk privately about returning for [[the-dragon-bones]] now that [[dakir]] — who disapproved — has gone, agreeing to reconstruct their route and perhaps enlist [[rell-aetris|Rell]]; this remains aspiration, not action. Fabian also conducts in-character "interviews" for a planned write-up.

A few days outside Greywatch, [[trevick|Trevick]] rejoins, having travelled faster alone with [[muriel]] and kept contact through [[dakir]]'s paired sending-tokens. He relays that Dakir has gone "to learn more about the dragons." The party catches Trevick up on the [[the-korrathian-conspiracy|Korrathian]] investigation: [[hethan-kroll|Kroll]] was a Furnace Brood agent (only him, not his men) sent to the Branweald to check whether the gate-crossing left a trace; the party traced his orders to a Bridgemarch cell, raided it, and recovered three scrolls naming three Brood operations, choosing the [[soren-dragan]]/Greywatch lead. In the telling, [[rell-aetris|Rell]] alone presses a theory that a **unicorn** tampered with the gate — the others do not share it ([[mags|Mags]] asks why they believe it). His basis is a centaur's account from the forest negotiations of a rare unicorn lately seen there; Rell, connecting dots the others miss, notes that unicorns are fey-native and magic-capable and have no place in the material forest, yet one appeared from nowhere in an area of a fresh [[the-feywild|Feywild]] crossing. Separately, and as news to [[fabian-perennius|Fabian]] and Mags, it comes out that the founding members ([[dakir]], Caspian, Trevick) had met two unicorns, [[stargleam]] and [[silverlily]], on the [[the-feywild-garden|Feywild Garden]] adventure (S1.04) — distinct from the unicorn behind Rell's theory. The catch-up also clarifies Torston's fate (see Key facts) and confirms the party now works for [[oscar-wicklow|Wicklow]] on a more permanent basis.

The DM then briefs the party (via history checks, with Fabian and Trevick best-informed) on [[the-mirrowen|the Mirrowen]] — the Tangles — and on [[greywatch]] itself: a magically-fragmented region, sacred to the [[itharis|Itharians]], effectively controlled by Trevaryn. The party arrives at the Mirrowen's edge on 28 Dusklin, a few days before the Itharian festival [[shadows-meet|Shadows Meet]] (1 Ithar's Veil), and finds the town overflowing with pilgrims. Wary of a possible mole in Wicklow's court, they agree not to use Fabian's family name or mention hunting a Korrathian ancient, settling on a treasure-seeker cover (Caspian as prospecting wizard, the others as his servants). They enter under false names, give "adventure" as their purpose, buy festival masks, and [[rell-aetris|Rell]] follows thieves'-cant markings to discreet, low-profile lodging. The session ends with the party settled in Greywatch, the contact's address in hand.

## Key facts learned
- **Benny triggered Torston's exposure** (confirmed): Benny reported the party's suspicions to the authorities after refusing Torston's overtures; he is now training with the Queen's Guard and holds official favour.
- **Torston's fate** (confirmed by the DM): he was detained for a time and his evidence confiscated (not withheld by choice), then reassigned to an "honourable" post in [[crownspire]] — a real reassignment ([[mags|Mags]]'s Sending confirmed him alive there), widely read by the party as a removal.
- **[[rell-aetris|Rell]]'s gate theory (his alone):** that a unicorn tampered with the gate. Basis: a centaur's report of an exceedingly rare unicorn lately seen in the forest, plus the reasoning that unicorns are fey-native, magic-capable, and shouldn't be there — appearing just where a [[the-feywild|Feywild]] crossing happened. The wider party doesn't share it. (The [[the-feywild-garden|Feywild Garden]] unicorns [[stargleam]]/[[silverlily]] are a separate matter.)
- **The Mirrowen (the Tangles)** is a region of fragmented planar reality: borderline habitable, psychically dangerous, full of aberration-like creatures and shifting land that mimics what it touches, with a high incidence of innate sorcerers. It is technically a League country but effectively a client of [[trevaryn|Trevaryn]], and is held sacred by the [[itharis|Itharians]].
- **Greywatch ("the Beacon")** is the only substantial town there — a walled, warded garrison town built (like [[drake-mount|Drake Mount]]) around old dragon-hospitality, with a signal-beacon tower watched from Trevaryn.
- The Itharian festival [[shadows-meet|Shadows Meet / the Vigil]] falls on 1 Ithar's Veil; masks are worn; overt Itharian priests are the lowest rank, and senior ones do not declare themselves. [[oscar-wicklow|Wicklow]] was an Itharian in youth and denies the priest rumours vehemently.
- The party now works for Wicklow on a more permanent basis, hunting the Korrathian cultists.
