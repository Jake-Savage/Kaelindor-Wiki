## Recap
A few weeks after their last mission, most of the party reside at Snowdrop House. Fabian receives an urgent summons from the minor noble Lord Torston and brings Caspian to meet him. Torston explains that Lord Edward Harrington was found drained of blood in the Silver Moon Bathhouse, and rumour blames a vampire. Torston and Harrington were rivals, and Torston fears the murder is not being properly investigated; he notes similar deaths south of the river among people with no family. He cannot offer a direct reward but promises payment for a slain vampire and gifts Fabian his grandfather's sunblade.

His bodyguard Cara confirms three or more similar deaths. The party learns Harrington was killed in a private room, arrived with a veiled woman who fled, and was found by attendants who broke in. Back at Snowdrop House, the party pools its knowledge of vampires. They decide to investigate the bathhouse before Mags can speak with the dead the next day.

At the Silver Moon Bathhouse, owner Gladys Henshaw lets them in. While Mags draws Gladys into conversation, the others search Harrington's room: Trevick finds scuff marks showing a skilled person climbed out the window, and Rell finds traces of blood flushed down the drain — odd, since vampires drain prey rather than discard blood. Staff reveal Harrington regularly visited with a woman named Nelly Fairchild, who works at The Floodgate pub. Gladys, meanwhile, reveals the body was displayed deliberately, neck wound exposed, and that Harrington had been boastful about a scheme he was close to pulling off. The party travels to The Floodgate to find Nelly.

## Key facts learned
- Lord Edward Harrington (human, middle-aged) was found drained in a private room at the Silver Moon Bathhouse; the killer left via the window, not the door.
- Blood was flushed down the drain — inconsistent with a vampire feeding.
- The body was deliberately displayed, unlike the hidden bodies of earlier victims south of the river.
- Harrington arrived with a veiled woman who fled the scene.
- Harrington regularly visited the bathhouse with Nelly Fairchild, who works at The Floodgate.
- Harrington had been boastful about a near-complete scheme.
- Lord Torston and Harrington were rivals/enemies; Torston commissions the hunt and gives Fabian a sunblade.

## Threads opened / advanced
- The bathhouse-vampire investigation is opened: who killed Harrington, and was it truly a vampire?
- The staged murder scene casts doubt on the vampire explanation from the outset.
- Harrington's mysterious scheme and his veiled companion become leads.
