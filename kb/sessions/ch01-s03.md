## Recap
Guided by the maps, the party find the ferryman Hadley, who tests them with Shalfay's passphrase ("Fair is the day... fairer by far the jewels of the evening"). Won over, Hadley reveals Shalfay is presumed dead and Pyarz now acts as High Mage, but the Tower's divinations have grown vague and riddling. He lays out the tower's layout and the gnome mercenary guards, the Griswald Garrison under Captain Maddock. At the lodging house the party question other petitioners — including Leonard Advardis, a sorcerer of a magical bloodline who lacks magic — and learn the divinations have been failing and that Pyarz hides his tattoos (which should change automatically with rank).

The party win over Captain Maddock, who smuggles them inside in mage's robes. Confronting Pyarz in the scholars' hall, Caspian exposes that Pyarz bears no rank tattoos — proof Shalfay must still be alive. In the ensuing battle, with some loyal mages turning to the party's side, Pyarz unleashes a spectator (beholder-kin) before Trevick lands the killing blow. They recover Pyarz's sending stone. Climbing to the observatory, they find the real Shalfay — haggard, clutching a great leatherbound divination book and threatening to burn it. They free him and prove his survival to the garrison and Maddock.

Shalfay reveals the star's true purpose: it is coveted by deep gnomes living near the surface who possess 10–12 "books of prophecy" beyond the scope of his own. He sends the party to trade the star for the books. At the gnomes' steam-powered forge, an automaton leads them to the gnome elders. With Comprehend Languages, the party learn the gnomes have spent sixty years collecting one of every type of rock to complete a coming-of-age rite; the star is their last rock, and they consider the prophetic books worthless. The trade is made gladly. The warden Eklash recognises the moss sample but treats it as taboo, revealing the gnomes have "a similar problem" in the under-dark.

## Key facts learned
- The Tower's divinations require the leatherbound book; Pyarz kept the petitioner's hall running without it, producing useless riddles.
- The deep gnomes traded their prophetic books for the star to complete a sixty-year rite of adulthood; they mention a war/corruption "we must resist."
- Dakir's construct nature was forced on him; the gnomes say he must have draconic ancestry to have his magic.
- The moss problem also exists in the under-dark; the gnomes believe (wrongly) it can't spread on the surface.
- Shalfay's sending stone, recovered from Pyarz, links to the shadow-blade's employer.

## Threads opened / advanced
- The fallen star — delivered to the gnomes; prophetic books obtained for Shalfay.
- Pyarz exposed and killed; Shalfay restored as High Mage.
- The shadow-blade's contract should now have ended with Pyarz's death.
- The moss / feywild contamination concern deepens.
