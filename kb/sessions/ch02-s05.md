## Recap
Summoned urgently to the estate, the party gives Torston a vague update and shares the news of Benny's claim. Torston is unbothered by the break-in and merely urges results. During the visit Mags slips away to scout the estate's guards, patrols, and entrances.

Returning home, Dakir and Trevick receive a message via enchanted tokens: Caspian has left the clocktower and is on his way back. Caspian is found shaken; Dakir berates him. Caspian reveals Lucien is a powerful vampire who had detained him and then, on leaving the city with Bartholomew, released him; he hands over the evidence (placing it on the table) along with artificer components Bartholomew left for Dakir. Caspian claims a book made him resistant to vampire charms. Mags is unconvinced and senses he is being evasive, but Calm Emotions shows no change.

The party examines the evidence: documents in Torston's hand concerning the Knights of the Silent Road (the Queen's elite), records of two lost knights (Myra Keln, Elar Trenwith), and accounts of tithed gold and magical items sent by airship — material that picks apart the official story of where war-tithe treasures came from. Torston is collating and sending these to someone in Crownspire. The party investigates the airship port (route: Bridgemarch → Mooncross → Crownspire) but cannot trace the packets. They search Lucien's now-empty lair under the clocktower (accessed by setting the clock to 9:50), recovering identity mementos, Damar's clothing, and 347 gold. They choose to let the Torston matter lie for now and look for work near Bridgemarch.

## Key facts learned
- Lucien left the city with Bartholomew, releasing Caspian whom he had detained; Caspian claims book-derived resistance to vampire charms.
- The evidence shows Torston falsifying records of the Knights of the Silent Road and the war-tithe of gold and magical items, sent by airship to Crownspire.
- The lair under the Brass Tower is opened by setting the clock to 9:50 ("0950").
- Airship route: Bridgemarch → Mooncross (Calredor) → Crownspire (Erythar); packets cannot be reliably traced.
- Lucien's clothing and identity mementos recovered; clothing later confirmed to be Damar's via tailor.

## Threads opened / advanced
- Lucien escapes the city; the "vampire hunt" is effectively unwinnable for a reward.
- Torston's treason is now evidenced but the buyer/destination in Crownspire is unconfirmed; party shelves it for later.
- Caspian's evasiveness deepens suspicion about what he learned/took from Lucien.
