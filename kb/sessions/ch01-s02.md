## Recap
Descending from Nerrin, the party come upon the bodies of four slain mages of the Order of the Tower of Heaven, killed by a memory web — a magical trap that harvested three memories from High Mage Shalfay: a vision of a star falling to earth ("scarred by the heavens"), and orders to seek the star and to take a beautiful Bestiary to a "fellow star-watcher," the druid Derwyth, in Cernant Valley. They take the Bestiary. That night a shadowy figure — a shadow blade mercenary with a dog and bird of shadow — interrogates Caspian and Dakir about the memories; both lie successfully and the figure departs.

At Stonehaven, the party report to Lady Aldara, who asks them to return Duvesin's signet ring to his descendants in Bridgemarch. Lucas gifts Trevick a magical crossbow in gratitude; the blacksmith Brynna Hammerfist takes a commission for owlbear armour and horn-caps. That night Dakir alone sees a star fall.

Reaching Cernant Valley, the party are welcomed by the giant eagles Avri and Ivo to the homestead of the elf druid Derwyth, who tests them before revealing herself from a tree. She identifies the memories' subject as High Mage Shalfay, warns of plots to oust him, and accepts the Bestiary as payment to locate the star. She directs them to a derro raiding camp (the derro are necromancers) at the star's crash site, near hunters at Lake Therro, and names the meddling hand of Rhynharran in the region's troubles. Trevick befriends Derwyth's sabretooth tiger Selice.

The party assault the derro camp, fighting derro and their zombies through flooded tunnels, and recover the star from the treasury along with gold-dipped antlers. They free a captive who proves to be a Lamia in disguise; she leaves peaceably to feed on the dead derro. Beyond a beaver-tended dam they reach the Mountain Village led by Aspen, whose people the derro had been raiding. The grateful villagers gift Trevick eighteen pearls.

## Key facts learned
- The fallen star is sought by Shalfay, who believes it "the key to the tower's future" and a defence against "evil seizing the power of prophecy."
- A shadow-blade mercenary hunts the same star, working for someone plotting against Shalfay from within the Tower.
- Derwyth is a self-exiled elf druid and "star-watcher"; she fled the elvish empire and won't discuss why.
- The derro are necromancers; the crater's curved tracks and excavation suggest the star was dug up.
- Rhynharran's "hand" is felt in the region's 18-month unrest.
