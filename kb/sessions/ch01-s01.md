## Recap
The party — Caspian Talon, the dragonborn sorcerer Dakir, and the beastmaster ranger Trevick with his owlbear Muriel — meet by chance in an inn in the market town of Stonehaven, drawn together by the innkeeper while seeking work offered by the city's ruler, Lady Aldara. They learn Aldara wishes to reopen the long-closed platinum mine at the abandoned mountain village of Nerrin to avoid raising war taxes.

Lady Aldara, with her son and heir Edric Vayne present, tasks them with determining whether Nerrin is fit to work and clearing out anything found there, for 750 gold. She recounts the town's history: seventy years ago a cave-in trapped and killed half of seventy miners; afterward, people began vanishing two or three at a time, until the village was found wholly deserted. She names two survivors now in Stonehaven — Lucas Grovener, innkeeper of the Bored Weasel, whose wife Lorna died in the cave-in, and Vaughn, a flower-seller who left as a child. Both describe a creeping terror that gripped the town. Lucas asks the party to lay flowers on Lorna's grave and retrieve a locket she hid in the town's offering tree, drawing them a map in thanks.

At Nerrin, Caspian and Trevick feel an unnatural dread. Hawk retrieves the locket. The party explore the mayor's house, finding the journals of Mayor Duvesin, which descend from clinical reports into madness as the disappearances mounted, plus books on the feywild and a map of the cave-in. Rats relate that a man was carried off by clawed creatures. The party camp down the mountain and return to enter the mine, where they find smooth tunnels, patches of black oily moss, and dark blood-stained stone slabs. In a brutal fight they destroy six meenlocks — fear-inducing creatures that drag victims to the slabs to convert them — saving Trevick and Dakir from the water and the slabs. Caspian's reading reveals the meenlocks nested from a feywild intrusion tied to a mountain tarn, triggered by the emotional trauma of the mine disaster. Trevick tends Lorna's grave, earning an unknowing blessing of Naelos.

## Key facts learned
- The Nerrin disappearances were caused by meenlocks, fey creatures that frighten victims and convert them into more meenlocks.
- A black, oily moss accompanies the meenlocks and is a sign of feywild intrusion.
- A mountain tarn above the mine has a thin boundary to the Feywild, especially at the full moon; strong emotional events can manifest a fey "nest" there.
- Mayor Duvesin's family bears a snowdrop signet ring; one was found on a meenlock corpse.
- Lady Aldara is pragmatic, academically curious, and intrigued by Dakir the construct.
