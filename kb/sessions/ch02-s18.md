## Recap
Lost and carrying the petrified Mags, the party stumbles onto a buried, magically preserved stretch of the Crossway extending deep into the forest — older than the Eldryn agreements. Following it, they are intercepted by five armed centaurs who recognise Mags's bark-kin venom as treatable and lead them to a large, hidden Eldryn encampment of centaurs and firbolgs. A robed centaur cures Mags with an antidote.

The wary leader, Hesk, and Captain Ethis tell the party the Eldryn are handling a forest crisis and will resolve it in two or three weeks, after which the party will be escorted out; they refuse to explain and forbid the party from learning anything. Hesk reluctantly agrees, after Fabian's argument, to send word to the town to prevent worse outsiders (like the mercenaries) from intervening. She demands an oath: not to leave the camp, make no effort to learn what's happening, and behave honourably. Most swear truthfully; Caspian, Rell, and Fabian initially lie (Fabian is caught and backs down). The party is housed with the two surviving Weathered, Reina and Elset, who hint the Eldryn regard the situation as a great tragedy and danger but won't say more.

## Key facts learned
- A buried, magically preserved stretch of the Crossway runs deep into the forest, predating the Eldryn agreements.
- The Eldryn camp is a temporary, hidden mobilisation of centaurs and firbolgs handling a forest crisis they expect to resolve in 2-3 weeks.
- Hesk leads (with Captain Ethis); she binds the party by oath not to leave or investigate, and reluctantly agrees to send word to the town.
- Centaurs carry bark-kin antidote; Mags is cured.
- The Weathered Reina and Elset are safe in the camp; the third (a messenger) died.

## Threads opened / advanced
- The Eldryn's secret crisis is set up as the heart of the uthrel mystery.
- The party is effectively detained under oath, raising the question of whether/how to learn the truth.
- Fabian secures a promise to warn the town.
