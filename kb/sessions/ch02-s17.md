## Recap
In a brutal fight at the faerie ring, the mist creature possesses Fabian and bark-kin shoot venom that "lignifies" (petrifies into wood) their targets — felling Mags, and beginning to take Caspian. Caspian realises destroying the ring would free the mist creature to roam, so they avoid that. Mags's desperate Lesser Restoration finally banishes the mist creature from Fabian, and in that moment Fabian manifests a new golden radiant magic that adds damage to his attacks — Caspian deduces it was triggered by Mags's selfless risk to save him.

The party drinks healing potions, drags the unconscious and the petrified clear, and flees the clearing disoriented and lost. During a short rest, Caspian recovers but Mags remains petrified; Caspian judges her condition non-fatal and treatable by Greater Restoration (or possibly Lesser Restoration with the right knowledge, or arcane magic). With Mags incapacitated, the party debates where to seek aid and resolves to push on to the Eldryn, who are closer than Greymere.

## Key facts learned
- Bark-kin venom petrifies victims into wood ("lignification"); Mags is fully petrified.
- The mist creature can possess a body; destroying the faerie ring would unbind it to roam freely.
- Fabian gains a golden radiant magic, triggered by Mags's selfless action to save him — closely integrated with his existing magic.
- Mags's petrification is non-fatal and curable (Greater Restoration; possibly Lesser Restoration or arcane magic with the right knowledge).
