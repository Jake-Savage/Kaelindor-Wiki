## Recap
The party joins a patrol of three centaurs and two firbolgs into the Hallow Burn, the burnt containment zone, observing how the Eldryn set and maintain anti-magic wards. They reach the uthrel's apparent origin: the ash where an infested tree stood, beside the blackened ruins of "Thalasyr Eren" — the Bright (or Moonlight) Gate, an ancient Feywild crossing sealed some 300-350 years ago when the elves fled the Feywild. Dakir argues the gate, not a random crossing, is the likely entry point for the uthrel.

Examining the gate's wards, Mags notices something off, and Caspian's detailed notes reveal a hidden second layer: someone deliberately altered the wards to pass through and then replaced them, relying on no one noticing the details. The patrol sets a watch, and back at camp Caspian and Mags privately convince a pale, shaken Hesk. She agrees it looks like the work of a stranger — not how the Eldryn use magic — and that other crossings must be checked. The Eldryn Council will convene early. Rell shows an uncharacteristic flash of excitement around the gate, which Mags notices.

## Key facts learned
- The uthrel's origin in the Hallow Burn sits right beside Thalasyr Eren (the Bright/Moonlight Gate), an ancient Feywild crossing sealed ~300-350 years ago.
- The gate's wards were deliberately tampered with — altered to allow passage, then restored — by someone whose magic is foreign to the Eldryn (a "stranger").
- The gate anchors the wards strengthening the planar barrier, which is why it wasn't destroyed.
- Hesk will convene the Eldryn Great Council early and post a permanent watch on the gate.
- Rell shows an unusual flash of excitement at the gate (noted by Mags).

## Threads opened / advanced
- The "who tampered with the gate" thread opens — an outsider deliberately let the uthrel through.
- The investigation must continue from Greymere, reaching out to magical contacts.
- Rell's odd reaction at the gate is a seeded suspicion.
