## Recap
At the mercenary camp, Fabian negotiates with Annet Brigg, who now leads the still-magically-loyal, leaderless band. He persuades them to return the 406 gold they extorted from the town in exchange for a writ to his brother Quintus Palaveus in Crownspire and 200 gold in travel expenses; the charmed mercenaries pack up and depart south, paying respects to Kroll's body. They report to Mathis (unhappy they leave before the crisis fully resolves) and prepare to push back to Bridgemarch to chase the Korrathian contact.

On the road, Fabian and Rell have a reflective conversation. Over successive days, Dakir repeatedly wakes exhausted and refuses to push hard; Mags grows suspicious that the tiredness is unnatural, but Dakir declines Rell's offer to inspect his dreams. The party splits: Mags, Rell, and Caspian press ahead while Fabian stays with the lagging Dakir. Fabian and Dakir bond over inns along the way — Dakir reveals he was once human, his magic descending from a dragon his ancestor met; Fabian shows off his sword-flute (a gift from his college mentor) and finally carves Dakir a passable wooden token for Fabian.

## Key facts learned
- Kroll's mercenaries remained magically loyal even after his death; Annet Brigg now leads them and accepts Fabian's writ-and-expenses deal, returning the town's gold and marching to Crownspire (to Quintus Palaveus).
- Dakir suffers recurring unnatural morning exhaustion on the road; Mags suspects something is wrong; Dakir refuses dream-inspection.
- Dakir was once human; his magic descends from a dragon an ancestor met, and arrived later in life than his siblings'.
- Fabian's sword-flute is a powerful magical focus, gifted by his college mentor.
