## Recap
The Greenheart, persuaded by the wren-token and the returned ring (which belongs to his third missing messenger, Reina), commissions Trevick to enter the forest to find the Eldryn and learn what happened to his people — secretly, since the official messengers never returned. Caspian's Identify confirms the ring is a Ring of Protection +1 with druidic abjuration magic.

The party sets off on a multi-day forest trek, Trevick navigating by Nessa's teaching. Off a side trail they find the missing hedge witch Illyra Fen hiding in a concealed dwelling. She admits she fled because, with elven blood, she feared being scapegoated for the fatal charm rather than judged on what she did. She reveals her charms work by skimming "little streams of magic" from the wild — the same magic the Yarrows feed on — and that this ambient magic has been drying up for months, growing harder to harvest. Caspian connects the diminishing magic to a thinning barrier with the Feywild. They leave Illyra in peace and make camp. That night a giant owlbear — far larger than Muriel — attacks; the party, using Fabian's owlbear plushie to confuse it into thinking it a cub, eventually lulls and drives it off.

## Key facts learned
- The ring is a Ring of Protection +1 (druidic), belonging to Reina, the Greenheart's missing third messenger.
- Illyra Fen fled fearing she'd be scapegoated for her heritage; her good-luck charm skims wild ambient magic — the same magic the Yarrows consume.
- The region's ambient magic has been drying up for months, making charm-making harder.
- Caspian links the fading magic to a thinning barrier between the Material Plane and the Feywild.
- Giant owlbears can grow far larger than Muriel.

## Threads opened / advanced
- Illyra found and exonerated in the party's eyes; the charm is incidental, not the true cause.
- The "missing magic" is tied to a Feywild barrier — a major clue toward the uthrel.
- The party presses on to find the Eldryn and the missing Weathered (Reina, Elset).
