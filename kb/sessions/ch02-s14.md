## Recap
At the marketplace, the party sees the mercenary leader Hethan Kroll and his band offering to "drive back" the Quickened for coin — an offer Lord Mathis has refused. Mags seeds rumours that they are scammers. At Serris's house, Mags and Rell learn from his parents, Elric and Berla, that Serris has suffered terrible recurring dreams of a monster for months — pre-dating the Quickened trouble — and is convinced Rhynharran needs an offering or sacrifice. Elric admits his own father had a reputation for foretelling bad weather through dreams, suggesting an ancestral gift. Serris first mentioned the dreams three months ago and has grown estranged from the temple, believing he knows more than they do. Mags searches his room and shrine but finds nothing magical.

At Nessa's cottage, the forest's response to Trevick's offering arrives: a soft-leather packet containing a carved wren (a symbol of welcome/favour, sacred to firbolgs), a silver-and-pale-wood ring (not sized for Trevick — meant for someone else), and an owl feather. Nessa explains someone in the forest deliberately reached out to Trevick. Separately, Trevick shows Dakir a note from "Joster" — a devil disguised as a tiefling whom they previously fought — referencing Trevick's father and enclosing his mother's silver pendant; they agree to keep it quiet for now. The party resolves to seek an audience with the Eldryn, going first to the Greenheart.

## Key facts learned
- Serris has had recurring prophetic dreams of a monster for ~3 months, pre-dating the Quickened crisis; his father's line foretold weather through dreams (ancestral gift).
- The mercenary band led by Hethan Kroll offers a violent solution; Mathis refused; they begin pressuring the townsfolk.
- The forest sent Trevick a carved wren (favour/welcome), a silver-and-wood ring (meant for another, not him), and an owl feather.
- "Joster" — a devil that posed as a tiefling — has contacted Trevick, citing his father and enclosing his late mother's pendant.

## Threads opened / advanced
