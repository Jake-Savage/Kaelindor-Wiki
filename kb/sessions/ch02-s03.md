## Recap
Past midnight, the party regroups and returns to Snowdrop House, taking Benny with them. Mags and Trevick decipher the documents. Benny's sealed letter is a signed, sealed deed naming him Harrington's natural son and heir — which Benny already knew. The deciphered letters, between Harrington ("EH") and a "Lucien," reveal a conspiracy against "Magnus" (Lord Torston): Damar (Torston's husband) is passing information under Lucien's charm; Elaine Blackward, Torston's swordhand, has secretly offered to meet Harrington; and Bartholomew Crane (a gnome, former paladin of Calvyr) is fortifying defences. The party deduces Lucien is a vampire and the documents implicate Torston as a traitor.

Leaving Benny hidden at Snowdrop House, the party spots a watcher on a nearby roof. After a chaotic magical scuffle, Fabian and Mags subdue the spy, Garron Corwen, who confesses he and Osric Crow watch the party on Torston's orders. Frightened more of Torston than of them, Garron agrees to feed Osric a bland account and tip the party off. He stages an escape as the city watch arrives.

The party goes to view Harrington's corpse, but Mags's Speak with Dead fails — someone already cast it. Brother Laurent points them to the watch, but the party decides Bartholomew is the better (and endangered) lead. At the Brass Tower clocktower, the frightened Bartholomew kills Theodore when he investigates, then agrees to talk. He confirms Torston is a traitor selling Kaelindor's secrets, that Harrington/Damar/Bartholomew gathered evidence, and strongly implies Lucien is a vampire whom he refuses to abandon. He asks for two days and refuses to explain "0950."

## Key facts learned
- Harrington's letters expose Torston ("Magnus") as a traitor selling state secrets; Damar (Torston's husband) leaked papers while under the vampire Lucien's charm.
- Benny is Harrington's legally acknowledged natural son and heir.
- Elaine Blackward/Blackthorne is Torston's swordhand and a courier of his secrets.
- Bartholomew Crane is a gnome ex-paladin of Calvyr, Harrington's co-conspirator, who runs the Brass Tower and is protecting the vampire Lucien.
- Harrington's corpse was already questioned via Speak with Dead before the party arrived.
- Garron Corwen and Osric Crow spy on the party for Torston in shifts.

## Threads opened / advanced
- The investigation pivots from "hunt a vampire" to "expose Torston's treason."
- Lucien the vampire is revealed as Bartholomew's protected friend, not a simple monster.
- Theodore (Caspian's familiar) is killed by Bartholomew.
- The meaning of "0950" remains a deliberate mystery.
