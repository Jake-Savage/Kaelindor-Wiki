## Recap
The party takes Benny to the College of Arms, where the heraldmaster Pellan begins processing the claim and Fabian vouches for him on his family name. Because legitimising Benny this way is irregular, it triggers a petition to the Queen. Back at Snowdrop House, amid party planning, Mags privately gets Benny drunk and — with Rell secretly reading his thoughts — learns Benny is not actually related to Harrington. The deed is genuine, though, so Harrington truly wanted Benny to inherit; Benny reasons it will all work out if no one learns the truth.

That night, Caspian slips away to meet Lucien in secret. The next morning he fails to appear at breakfast; Trevick finds Caspian's door locked from inside and his revealing notes within. A messenger delivers a letter from Caspian and a terrified Theodore: Caspian has gone to see Lucien and been "invited" to remain until the vampire leaves town. Even after Mags casts Augury, the party spends a long time debating whether to pursue him, with Dakir cautious and Mags adamant.

## Key facts learned
- Benny is not Harrington's biological son, but Harrington's signed deed naming him heir is genuine.
- Legitimising Benny requires a petition to the Queen and will become public, high-profile news.
- Caspian secretly arranged to meet Lucien and has been detained by the vampire.
- Theodore survived (returned terrified) — Caspian's familiar.

## Threads opened / advanced
- Benny's inheritance quest formally begins via the College of Arms petition.
- Caspian's reckless solo dealings with Lucien open a thread of his hidden agenda and possible compromise.
