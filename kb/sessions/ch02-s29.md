## Recap
The party spends a full day in [[greywatch]] working the [[soren-dragan]] address, splitting into
two groups. [[mags]], [[fabian-perennius|Fabian]] and [[trevick]] go into the festival-crowded
streets to gather gossip; [[caspian-talon|Caspian]] and [[rell-aetris|Rell]] stake out the address
itself.

Working the crowds, Mags steers conversation toward local powers and the address. The party learns
[[greywatch]] is ruled by an elected **Warden of the Beacon**, chosen from a council of fourteen
for a twelve-year term; the current Warden, **[[ivo-pierron|Ivo Pierron]]**, is in his last year,
and his successor must hold the approval of [[trevaryn|Trevaryn]]'s ruler, **[[lord-valcarin|Lord
Valcarin]]**. Townsfolk assume the [[itharis|Itharian]] Church controls the council but acknowledge
no formal tie, naming "two powers" in the [[the-mirrowen|Mirrowen]] — the Church and
[[drake-mount|Drake Mount]]. Dropped into conversation, the address is readily recognised: it is
the [[the-three-copper-hatch|Three Copper Hatch]], the cheap-meal business of **[[jorrith-potter|Jorrith
Potter]]**, a widowed cook in his fifties. Further gossip paints him as well-liked but pitied — he
left town for a decade, returned with his wife **[[meera]]** to set up the business, and has carried
on wearily alone since she died of a quick illness years ago.

Concurrently, Caspian (Invisibility) and Rell stake out the hatch. Rell, walking the street under
Detect Thoughts, probes for a concealment field like the one at the Bridgemarch
[[the-tiefling-shop|shop]]; he finds nothing as blunt as that, but cannot rule out something
subtler. He then moves rooftop-to-rooftop to a vantage and opens a long telepathic link to Caspian.
Caspian watches Jorrith cook, open at midday, and serve poorer locals two cheap dishes a day, one
meal each; no customer shows agent-like behaviour, and Detect Magic at the hatch does not flare.

Mags, Fabian and Trevick come to the hatch for lunch; Caspian shadows them invisibly nearby, while
Rell stays at his hidden vantage and does not approach. Beforehand, Mags hands Trevick one of her
bones to plant on Jorrith so they can locate him later. At the side door, while Mags makes small
talk, Trevick goes
for the pocket clumsily and Jorrith briefly suspects a pickpocket; Trevick recovers with a charm
offensive in a Travarian accent, Jorrith softens, and the bone is placed. Mags then drops the word
"brood" into a sentence and watches: Jorrith gives no dramatic reaction, only a faint flicker and a
beat-too-long look before his warmth fades to something guarded — read by Mags as not fear but the
weight of a private burden. Caspian, still invisible, drops cover afterward to quietly praise
Trevick's save without telling the others he nearly blew it.

While Caspian and Rell continue the watch, the gossip group spends the afternoon at the
[[the-pindle-brak|Pindle Brak]], a gnome pub. Fabian surprises everyone by speaking fluent Gnomish
and, with songs and tales of his mentor, wins over the put-out regulars (Mags, unusually, helps).
Trevick drinks the festival spirit "the bad idea" and rides out its perception-altering effects in
good humour; Fabian recalls that such liminal-state brews are typical of Itharian rites. The gnomes'
gossip yields little hard intelligence: enthusiasm for the festival, firm belief in the Mirrowen's
identity-mimics outside the walls (and a culture of vouching for travellers), no knowledge of secret
local cults, and nothing new about dragons. Mags later sobers Trevick with Lesser Restoration.

Caspian, meanwhile, sends his familiar [[theodore|Theodore]] into Jorrith's house: it is tidy,
spartan, and clean, with two rooms and a privy below and a single bedroom above — no shrine,
sigils, or hidden doors like the Bridgemarch house, only a framed drawing of a young woman (Meera,
though Caspian does not know her). His own outside survey of the building maps the alley, back door,
yard, and windows. Probing again for a masking field yields only the same inconclusive result.

That evening the party settles its plan: rather than approach Jorrith as themselves, Rell will
impersonate **[[soren-dragan|Soren]]**, the Brood agent whose dispatch they intercepted in
Bridgemarch, and collect the orders Soren was meant to receive. They reason the real Soren never got
his instructions (the party took them) and so will not appear, and that the dispatch named no
codeword; the "rumour" Soren was sent to chase, they already know, concerns a village called
[[kethons-fold|Kethon's Fold]]. Rell links the party with a telepathic spell he passes off as his
own creation (in truth his disguised innate sorcery), the
others take hidden positions to cover the exits, and Rell — Disguise Self for an androgynous look,
over a physical adventurer's costume — approaches the quiet hatch.

Rell addresses Jorrith by name and, mid coin-trick, flashes the Furnace Brood sigil with
Prestidigitation, then produces the [[amulet-of-proof-against-detection]] as the second half of the
sign. Jorrith (a blank to Detect Thoughts, as if mind-shielded) loses colour, panics at the openly
shown amulet, and ushers "Soren" inside. There, terrified and nearly collapsing, he delivers the
Brood's orders: travel about forty miles south to Kethon's Fold, a village swallowed by a sinkhole
some ten weeks ago; enter the caves said to lead underground (where some claim to have heard singing
or crying); go as deep as is safe; and bring back anything strange that "does not belong," so the
Brood can judge whether to send more people. Reading Rell's feigned weariness, Jorrith shows sympathy
and asks "who did they take from you?" — the Brood coerce their agents by holding loved ones
hostage, and Jorrith is held the same way — and admits this is the first time he has had to do this. Rell deflects, accepts the task, and
leaves; Caspian watches (through Theodore) Jorrith close up, tidy obsessively, and lie down upstairs
as if having a panic attack.

The party agrees not to turn Jorrith in — he seems a coerced, decent man, and they want him as a
live link to the Brood — and resolves to reach Kethon's Fold with an unofficial guide (not the
recorded guild) to stay off the record, skipping the festival, which is only days off. The session
ends with the party preparing to leave Greywatch.

## Key facts learned
- **The contact is [[jorrith-potter|Jorrith Potter]]** (confirmed): the cook of the
  [[the-three-copper-hatch|Three Copper Hatch]] is the [[the-furnace-brood|Furnace Brood]]'s
  Greywatch contact — he recognised the recognition sign, took "Soren" inside, and delivered Brood
  orders. He reads **blank to Detect Thoughts** (personal mind-shielding), but his house carries
  **no blunt dampening field** like the Bridgemarch shop, no shrine, sigils, or hidden doors.
- **The Brood's orders** (confirmed, via Jorrith): go ~40 miles south to **[[kethons-fold|Kethon's
  Fold]]** — destroyed ~10 weeks ago by a sinkhole — enter underground via reported caves (stories
  of singing/crying below), go as deep as is safe, recover anything anomalous that "doesn't belong,"
  and report back so the Brood can decide whether to send more people.
- **The Brood coerce agents via hostages** (confirmed): they hold agents' loved ones as leverage,
  and Jorrith himself is held by a **living hostage** (identity unknown) — separate from the gossip
  that his late wife [[meera]] died of illness.
- **Greywatch's governance** (DM-confirmed): an elected **Warden of the Beacon**
  ([[ivo-pierron|Ivo Pierron]], in his final year) chosen from a council of fourteen for a 12-year
  term; the successor needs the approval of [[trevaryn|Trevaryn]]'s ruler [[lord-valcarin|Lord
  Valcarin]] (elected by eight families for 25 years). The Itharian Church is assumed to dominate
  the council but holds no formal tie; "two powers" — the Church and Drake Mount — are seen to run
  the Mirrowen.
- **Festival lore** (DM-confirmed): [[shadows-meet|Shadows Meet]]'s night unfolds at unannounced
  places around town; anyone may join the public celebration; perception-altering brews like the
  gnomes' "the bad idea" are typical of Itharian rites. The god **[[vraxyn|Vraxyn]]** (rendered
  "Braxen"/"Braxton") is regarded as a problem, with temples banned across Kaelindor but not in
  Crownspire.
- **Rell's telepathic link** (capability confirmed): up to three people, ~1 mile, ~1 hour, working
  as a relay through Rell. He presents it as an original spell he created on the road, but this is
  part of his **wizard disguise** — it is really his innate (Aberrant Mind) sorcery.
