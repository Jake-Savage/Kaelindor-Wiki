## Recap
Honouring the oath in spirit while testing its edges, the party spends the day among the Eldryn. Reina explains the Eldryn distrust outsiders with forest secrets and that the forest is ancient and once far larger. The party covertly gathers intelligence: Rell counts ~50+ in 6-8 person patrols with a high proportion of spellcasters; Dakir helps with chores and judges supplies thin and the camp hastily improvised; Caspian (through an invisible Theodore) overhears planning about placing magic and confirms a heavy stock of warding components used to create barriers.

Trevick, showing his wren-token to the firbolgs, is welcomed warmly. When he summons Hawk, the firbolgs react with reverence: they call Hawk a "Watcher," a concentrated spirit Rhynharran sends to watch over those who must witness or do something important — and they say it has watched Trevick's family across generations, warning that this is both an honour and a burden. Trevick begins to sense Hawk is no ordinary bird. Caspian later persuades Trevick to let Hawk track the patrols to find where the wards are being placed.

## Key facts learned
- The Eldryn are placing many anti-magic / barrier wards and searching a burnt zone; supplies are thin and the camp is improvised, reacting to an unplanned crisis.
- The camp holds ~50+ Eldryn in patrols, with an unusually high proportion of spellcasters.
- The firbolgs revere Hawk as a "Watcher" — a spirit of Rhynharran that watches a family across generations over matters of great importance; it is both honour and burden.
- The forest is ancient and was once far larger than today.

## Threads opened / advanced
- Trevick's Watcher (Hawk) is revealed as a spirit of Rhynharran tied to his lineage and destiny.
- The Eldryn's anti-magic warding effort and the burnt western zone point directly at the uthrel.
- Hawk is set to scout the patrols, advancing the party's covert investigation.
