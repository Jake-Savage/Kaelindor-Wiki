## Recap
At Queen Rowenna's monthly Hearing Day, two villagers from Toadhop — escorted by the dragonborn fighter Sidney Pollard — petition for help: the grave of the beloved bard Frederick Dartwell has been robbed and his magical golden lute stolen. The young bard Fabian Perennius volunteers his services. The party — now Caspian, Dakir, Trevick, Sidney, Fabian, the goliath paladin Orin Njord, and the gnome cleric Mags (here introduced as Orin's "aunt") plus the masked Tir'Van Relldayn ("Rell") — set off to Toadhop.

Investigating the grave, Mags exhumes and inspects the bones (minor bones missing, clawed scratch marks); Caspian finds small clawed footprints. The local suspects Todd Macby and Jory Pike prove innocent of the lute, but a terrified Jory describes five or six small green clawed creatures digging up the grave and vanishing. The party deduce quasits — little demons that serve a more powerful master. At the inn, a freed footman Corbin Dunn reveals Lord Adilaire of Paliset Hall has grown reclusive since his wife's death, holding secretive parties, and that a tiefling named Joster Sirit arrived three months ago, dismissed the local guards, and tightened security.

Posing as a performance troupe, the party infiltrate Paliset Hall. Rell (disguised) and an invisible Caspian and Dakir discover Lord Adilaire under magical thrall, sluggishly signing away his lands to a "Nymara Foss" under the supervision of a valet. They confront Joster, who is revealed as a devil — collecting magical artifacts and using enchanted portraits to drain his subjects. A vicious battle ensues with Joster and two shadow demons; Joster recognises Trevick as "a Salmeros child" and pointedly notes his absent father, without claiming to have known the man. Dakir's wand of lightning bolts finally drives Joster back to the hells. Freed, Adilaire thanks them. The party find Joster's extradimensional hoard containing the golden lute and much loot, which they take. They return the perpetrator-guards to Bridgemarch jail, gift the Toadhop villagers 500 gold (claiming the lute too dangerous to keep), and let Fabian claim the golden lute for his college.

## Key facts learned
- Quasits served Joster Sirit, a devil disguised as a tiefling, who robbed the grave for the lute.
- Joster enthralled Lord Adilaire to strip his lands and assets (transferring ownership to "Nymara Foss"), using magical portraits to feed.
- Joster recognised Trevick as "a Salmeros child" — a name he said he had not heard in a long time — and pointedly remarked that no boy should grow up without a father; he did not claim to have known the man. A thread of Trevick's heritage.
- The party keeps 2,500 gold from the hoard plus an 800 gold reward.

## Threads opened / advanced
- Golden lute recovered; quest resolved (lute kept by Fabian/his college).
- Trevick's parentage — Joster's recognition of the Salmeros name seeds a future mystery.
- Joster banished to the hells, not destroyed.
