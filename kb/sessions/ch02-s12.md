## Recap
Caspian, Fabian, Dakir, and Rell go to Rhynharran's Hall and, through Hall-Keeper Josselin Feld, meet the Greenheart Thalos Briarwood — an old, formal man who treats even Dakir without distaste. Thalos counsels only patience, insisting balance always returns, and refuses to disclose where the Eldryn are. He retells the Stormblind legend (a great unnatural storm centuries ago that swallowed a praying expedition without a trace) and admits he has sent messengers to the Eldryn that have not returned. He permits the party to observe the Quickened at dusk with the druid Anwen Ireth.

Meanwhile Mags and Trevick visit Nessa's cottage. Nessa, a firbolg-blooded hedge witch and potion-maker, agrees to guide them into the forest. She reveals the forest's trail markers have been changing faster and more unpredictably for two seasons, that more areas are now forbidden, and that in spring she smelled faint smoke from a distant forest fire to the west — around the time the markers grew unstable. She speaks warmly of the missing Illyra Fen, noting Illyra has elven blood and had long planned to leave.

## Key facts learned
- The Greenheart Thalos Briarwood counsels patience, hides the Eldryn's location, and his sent messengers have not returned.
- The Stormblind storm centuries ago was unnatural; the lost expedition left no bodies or wreckage.
- Nessa: the forest markers have been unstable for ~two seasons; many more areas are now off-limits.
- Nessa smelled a distant fire to the west in spring, coinciding with the markers becoming unstable.
- Illyra Fen has elven blood and had long intended to leave; Nessa trusts she is safe.

## Threads opened / advanced
- The western fire is established as a key clue to the deeper cause.
- Arranged dusk observation of the Quickened with Anwen Ireth.
- Nessa becomes the party's forest guide and a trusted contact.
