## Recap
Centaurs escort the party and the Weathered back toward Greymere; on the road, the centaurs claim to have seen a unicorn but disbelieve the party's tales of meeting unicorns and dragons. Caspian uses Sending to the Tower of Heaven and learns the uthrel name was helpful but the books the party donated are somehow "off" — wrong — prompting talk of a future trip there.

At the forest edge they find Nessa's cottage closed and locked as if abandoned. In town, tension is high: there are no proper guards (just nervous deputies), the streets are purposeful and quiet, and the mercenary leader Hethan Kroll has been murdered in his camp in the dead of night. Splitting up, Dakir and Rell scout the mercenary camp while Fabian and Caspian escort the Weathered to Rhynharran's Hall, finding Nessa sheltering there among townsfolk to avoid being scapegoated. The Greenheart, Briarwood, confirms the centaur messenger Vasha arrived, helped face down Kroll's mercenaries, then went to Lord Mathis; Fabian suspects Briarwood knows who killed Kroll. Dakir slips invisibly into Kroll's untouched tent, finds it bears no sign of struggle (a single fatal blow), and takes records and a locked box; Rell struggles to open the unexpectedly complex lock.

## Key facts learned
- The Tower of Heaven reports the books the party donated are somehow "wrong"/off — a new puzzle worth a trip.
- Nessa's cottage is abandoned; she has sheltered at Rhynharran's Hall to avoid being targeted as an outsider.
- Hethan Kroll was murdered in his camp by a single blow, his tent left untouched and unplundered.
- The centaur messenger Vasha reached Greymere, faced down the mercenaries, then went to Mathis; she is later found still at the Green Hall (S2.24).
- Dakir recovers Kroll's mercenary records and a complex locked box.

## Threads opened / advanced
- Kroll's murder and the unplundered tent (ritualistic?) become a mystery.
- The locked box (opened next session) ties Kroll to a larger conspiracy.
- The "wrong books" thread at the Tower of Heaven opens.
