## Recap
Following Trevick through the forest, the party detours to a "place of remembrance" where Dakir feels his draconic magic resonate with something ambient. Among flowers they uncover the partially buried bones of a dragon — Dakir judges it a young silver dragon, closely related to his own bronze lineage. The flowers are a type of Quickened attuned to the grove's ambient magic; when Dakir lays hands on the bones they animate as dancing flower-creatures, which the party fights and Dakir ultimately subdues by manipulating the resonant magic. An argument erupts over harvesting the bones; Dakir takes three (wing, claw, tooth), makes Caspian erase his notes, and lets the grove settle, aware he has disrupted its magic.

Pressing on, the trail leads to a withered faerie ring of shrivelled mushrooms — recognised as a place where the planar barrier is thin, marked by an unfamiliar mould. Caspian links it to where magic may be "going." As Dakir tests it with Mage Hand, a mist creature takes form inside the ring and drains his magic, while bark-kin and "hanging fellow" ambush predators attack from the trees. Combat erupts.

## Key facts learned
- The grove holds the magic-rich bones of a young silver dragon, resonant with Dakir's draconic power; Dakir takes a wing, claw, and tooth.
- The grove's flowers are Quickened attuned to its ambient magic; disturbing the dragon disrupts that magic.
- A withered faerie ring marks a thin point in the planar barrier, with an unfamiliar mould — a possible site where ambient magic is being lost.
- Fey ambush creatures here: a mist creature, bark-kin, and "hanging fellows."

## Threads opened / advanced
- Dakir's draconic heritage thread deepens; the dragon bones become significant to him.
- The withered faerie ring and unfamiliar mould foreshadow the uthrel and the thinning Feywild barrier.
- The party is drawn into a dangerous fey ambush (resolved next session).
