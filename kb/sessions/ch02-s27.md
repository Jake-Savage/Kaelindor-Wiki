## Recap
Before the party returns to the Korrathian [[the-tiefling-shop|shop]], Dakir informs the group that he is leaving. He states he is struggling with his identity and has questions to answer, including learning more about dragons after the remains encountered in the Eldryn forest. He wishes the party well and says he hopes to meet them again. (See [[dakirs-draconic-heritage]].)

The remaining party returns to the [[the-tiefling-shop]], where the city guards continue to search; the magic dampener is still active. They go first to the chests, as Caspian has since learned Dispel Magic. His first attempt fails and injures him; a second attempt, assisted by Mags and Fabian, succeeds. After Fabian fails to lift one box, Mags has the guards carry it outside, where she opens it. The contents are further trapped.

Rell investigates the contents, focusing on a set of protected documents. Augmented by Mags, he determines that the protective magic can be circumvented but not how to do so immediately. Caspian studies the magic and shares what he finds; together they confirm that Rell's [[amulet-of-proof-against-detection|amulet]] will help — it does not disable the traps, but makes the ward less able to register Rell as the wrong recipient. With it, Rell gets all the documents open. The party tells the guards they are taking the papers for study and will share the results; Caspian writes a receipt and promises a translation.

Searching the rest of the property, Fabian — guided by Mags — traces the dampening effect to the kitchen, beneath the flagstones, and has the guards and Rell lever up the stone. Beneath it is a blackened stone carved with magical runes. Moving it makes the rituals worked here evident, leaving the area's magical aura nauseating. Fabian and Rell find scratches and other physical signs. Rell uses Detect Thoughts for mental or emotional imprints and finds psychic traces of emotion — a general sense of suffering — but cannot resolve detail. The residual magic and the suffering cause both Rell and Caspian physical pain, which Rell withstands better.

Caspian identifies the magics used: communication magic and the renewal of bonds, oaths, or pledges of service and allegiance, and possibly exchange, tribute, or offerings — and reads some of the suffering as having been inflicted for research alone. Rell projects the residual emotions and memories into the room for the others; Fabian and Mags perceive them, while Caspian declines further exposure. Rell presses for clearer memories, the pain intensifies, and after Mags heals him and a third attempt fails, he stops before it can kill him.

Back at [[snowdrop-house]], the party translates the papers — three Furnace Brood instruction-scrolls:
- The first is addressed to [[hethan-kroll]]: travel south by water through Offendale to a town, Fairfield, and to the manor of a noble, Amir Merillac, who has been working for the [[the-furnace-brood|Furnace Brood]] but whom the Brood now suspects. Kroll is to bring his best people as security and to watch for the noble double-crossing the Brood.
- The second is for Tamzin Venik, apparently a travelling merchant: head north to Rivers Meet and then to the mountains, openly and not in secret, carrying five magical tokens that act as beacons to those who know them; meet "the hunters," verify they have placed caches, and leave one token in each cache.
- The third is for Soren Dragan, apparently an adventurer or treasure-hunter: travel overland through northern Offendale to the Tangles (Meroen) and meet a contact in Greywatch to pursue a rumour, quickly and in secret, with the work expected to be dangerous. An address is enclosed.

The party resolves to pursue Soren Dragan to [[greywatch]]. They check in briefly with [[lord-torston|Torston]] to ask whether his reported new position is genuine and whether he has access to the evidence of a conspiracy; Torston says the investigation should continue but does not comment on the position or the evidence.

## Key facts learned
- Dakir leaves the party to pursue his own questions about his nature and about dragons.
- The chests beneath the Korrathian shop held trapped, magically protected documents; Rell opened them using his amulet, with Caspian's and Mags's help.
- A blackened, rune-carved stone beneath the shop's kitchen was the source of the magic dampener and the site of the rituals.
- Caspian's reading of the ritual magic: communication; renewal of oaths/pledges of service and allegiance; possible tribute or offerings; and suffering inflicted partly for research. Rell's Detect Thoughts found only a general residue of suffering.
- The three translated scrolls name three Furnace Brood agents and assignments: Hethan Kroll → the noble Amir Merillac near Fairfield, Offendale (suspected of double-crossing the Brood); Tamzin Venik → placing beacon-tokens in caches north toward the mountains, openly; Soren Dragan → a contact in Greywatch (the Tangles/Meroen), to pursue a rumour, secretly.
- Torston neither confirmed nor denied a reported new position or access to the conspiracy evidence; he said only that the investigation should continue.
