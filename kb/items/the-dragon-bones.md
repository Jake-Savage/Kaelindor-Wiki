## Description
Three bones — a wing, a claw, and a tooth — taken from the partially buried remains of a young silver dragon, a lineage closely related to [[dakir|Dakir]]'s own bronze draconic magic. The bones are magic-rich and resonate with Dakir's power. They are emotionally and arcanely significant to him: as a [[constructs|construct]] uncertain of his origin, Dakir was deeply moved by the silver dragon's remains.

## History
The party detoured to a "place of remembrance" — the [[the-dragon-grove|dragon grove]] — where [[dakir|Dakir]] felt his magic resonate with something in the ground; they uncovered the buried young silver dragon (S2.16). The grove's flowers were [[the-quickened|Quickened]] attuned to its ambient magic and animated to fight the party when Dakir touched the bones; Dakir subdued them by manipulating the resonant magic. Over an argument about harvesting, Dakir took the wing, claw, and tooth, made [[caspian-talon|Caspian]] erase his notes, and let the disrupted grove settle. The bones tie directly into his draconic-heritage thread: he later revealed his magic descends from a dragon an ancestor met, and that he was once human (S2.25).
