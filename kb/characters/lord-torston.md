## Overview
Lord Magnus Torston is a minor Bridgemarch noble who hires the party (through [[fabian-perennius]]) to hunt the "vampire" behind the bathhouse death of his rival [[edward-harrington]] (S2.01). He presents as a wronged, frustrated patron — gifting Fabian the [[sunblade-of-torston]] and urging a proper investigation. His estate (the [[torston-estate]]) is well-guarded; his bodyguard is [[cara]], his swordhand and courier is [[elaine-blackthorne]], and his husband is [[damar]].

## Knowledge & motivations
Torston is a man of two faces. Publicly he is the aggrieved rival; privately he is a **traitor** falsifying records of the [[knights-of-the-silent-road]] and the wartime tithe of gold and magical items, collating the material and shipping it by airship to a buyer in [[crownspire]] (S2.05). His genuine, unfeigned tenderness is reserved for [[damar]]: when he learns a member of his household has been charmed, he is "fiercely protective," leads the party to his ailing husband, and embraces him when Mags briefly breaks the charm (S2.06). He is fixated, for unknown reasons, on the Queen's inner circle. He is unbothered by the break-in at his home and merely presses the party for results — confident in his own concealment.

## Appearances
- S2.01: Hires the party; gifts the sunblade; the bathhouse killing is presented as a vampire kill (a frame).
- S2.03: His treason is exposed in Harrington's deciphered letters ("Magnus"); his spies [[garron-corwen]]/[[osric-crow]] watch the party.
- S2.05: Summons the party; the airship/Crownspire evidence is laid out.
- S2.06: Admits Damar was "affected"; shown as a man protecting his husband.
- S2.09: Trevick's stolen study papers tie him to prominent Crownspire figures — suspicious but not conclusive.
- S2.10: Publicly buries the hatchet once [[benny-carter]]'s claim succeeds; the matter is shelved.

## DM notes / secrets
- **Torston's confirmed crime is treason, not murder.** The bathhouse killing was staged as a vampire feeding (body displayed, neck exposed, blood flushed down the drain — inconsistent with a real vampire) to discredit the investigation, but who actually killed Harrington is never established in play. Suspicion falls on Torston only because (a) [[bartholomew-crane]] believed Torston was behind it and (b) Torston's courier [[elaine-blackthorne]] was the veiled woman at the scene — suspicion, not proof. Harrington was building a treason case against him and meant to take it to Princess [[princess-orlaith|Órlaith]], which is the motive the party reads into the staging.
- He is a **traitor selling state secrets** to a buyer in Crownspire (unproven in-world; the party shelved the case). The buyer/destination remains the unresolved core of the `expose-torston` thread.
- He falsified the records of the [[knights-of-the-silent-road]] (lost knights Myra Keln, Elar Trenwith) and the war-tithe accounting.
