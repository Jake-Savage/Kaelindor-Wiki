## Overview
Fabian Perennius is a human Bard who joins the party at Queen Rowenna's Hearing Day to recover the [[the-golden-lute]] (S1.05). The seventh son of a wealthy [[crownspire]] dynasty (linked to the [[the-advardis-family]]), he is vain, theatrical, and forever casting the party's deeds as the heroic story that will make his name. He wields [[the-golden-lute]] and a magical sword-flute (his college mentor's gift), briefly carried the [[sunblade-of-torston]], and uses a [[brooch-of-shield]]. He gives [[trevick]] the owlbear plushie [[beakers]], whose "cub" ruse turns aside a giant owlbear (S2.15).

## Knowledge & motivations
Fabian craves comprehensible, conquerable evil and a decisive epic confrontation: he is frustrated by Greymere's slow druids and uninterpretable [[the-quickened|Quickened]], pining for "an evil mastermind pulling the strings" he can confront (S2.12). He is keenly competitive with [[hethan-kroll]]'s mercenaries, fearing they will "seize the moment and define the narrative" (S2.14).

His defining motivation is legacy and museum-fame: as a boy he stood beneath the first dragon skeleton returned to Crownspire (the Aelorin Vaelthas Stonemarch Expedition) and resolved that those who bring back wonders "would never be forgotten." He fantasises a "Perennius Gallery of Eldarin-Draconic Relations" built around the silver-dragon bones and conspires with [[caspian-talon]] to relocate them (S2.16). Yet he is also more decent than his background: [[mags]] freeing him from the mist creature rather than healing herself genuinely moves him — "That was real heroism" (S2.17) — and that selfless act triggers his new **golden radiant magic**. His Nameweave communion with [[rell-aetris]] (S2.20) is "a kind of beauty Fabian has not experienced before."
