## Overview
Dakir is a Sorcerer of bronze draconic lineage and a founding party member (with [[caspian-talon]] and [[trevick]], S1.01). He is thoughtful, balance-seeking, and reluctant to shed blood — repeatedly favouring restraint with the [[the-quickened|Quickened]], the giant owlbear, and the [[the-eldryn]]. He carries a [[wand-of-lightning-bolts]] (which banished the devil [[joster-sirit]], S1.05) and a [[dakirs-spell-storing-device]] installed in his body. He is uniquely advanced among the rare [[constructs]] of the world.

## Knowledge & motivations
Dakir does not fully understand his own power: "It's like I'm just scratching the surface" (S2.15). The silver-dragon bones in [[the-dragon-grove]] are the emotional centre of his arc — their magic feels "different yet familiar," and attuning to them "opened my eyes to the possibilities I may one day reach for" (S2.16). He secretly takes three bones (wing, claw, tooth → [[the-dragon-bones]]), intending to consult the [[church-of-calvyr]], and is grateful [[trevick]] stopped him distributing more. He longs for belonging — his Nameweave reflections describe "Dakir's long search for belonging" and a wealth-obsessed family whose Nameweave was all selfish machination (S2.18–20).

He approaches the Greymere crisis as a puzzle of disrupted balance, reasoning early that the party must go "beyond the boundary" to the [[the-eldryn]] (S2.12), and he is the one who argues the [[the-bright-gate]] — not a random crossing — is the Uthrel's entry point (S2.22). He values the party's diplomatic approach and credits patience for their breakthroughs.

## DM notes / secrets
- **Dakir has left the party (S2.27)** to pursue [[dakirs-draconic-heritage|his own questions]] about his nature and dragons; he is no longer an active member. His unresolved exhaustion (below) leaves with him.
- **Dakir is a Church-of-Calvyr construct, roughly ten years old**, not a born dragonborn. He himself frames it as "I was once human; my magic descends from a dragon an ancestor met" (S2.25) — his understanding of his own nature is partial. His draconic magic arrived later in life than his siblings'. The [[church-of-calvyr]] made him roughly ten years ago (the place is never stated); separately and later, its artificer-priest [[talan-pryce]] — using components left by [[bartholomew-crane]] — fitted his spell-storing device (S2.10). Talan and Bartholomew relate to the *device*, not his original making.
- His **late unexplained exhaustion** (S2.25) — waking drained, refusing dream-inspection — is an open, unresolved hook. It may be tied to his construct nature, the dragon bones, or something else; deliberately left a mystery.
