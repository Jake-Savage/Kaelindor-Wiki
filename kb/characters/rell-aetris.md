## Overview
Rell — Relldayn Aetris — is a masked, reclusive member of the [[the-tir-van|Tir'Van]] ("Horizonborn") who joins the party for the Toadhop job (S1.05). He is known publicly as a wizard and has pointedly never described a particular school or subclass — a deliberate cover (see DM notes). He wields mind magic — telepathy and psionic power — with mind-reading his standby (Detect Thoughts in every investigation); he keeps his face and history hidden and refuses to swear oaths. He carries a flawed [[amulet-of-proof-against-detection]]. Among the [[the-tir-van]] he is one of a many-eyed people who commune wordlessly, mind to mind, rather than through speech.

## Knowledge & motivations
Rell's overriding goal is to find a way home and to the rest of his people. His diary frames the Tir'Van as exiles cut off behind a sealed [[the-horizon-doors|Horizon Door]] — his elders speak of "a battle for survival" and an exodus, and he wonders whether [[the-uthrel]] ("this parasitic moss, this arcanophage") is the corruption that drove them out, "the first true step toward my goal I have seen" (S2.20). When the party finds an intact, still-active [[the-bright-gate|gate]], his reaction is rapture: "It is beyond our greatest hope... a thinking force can step between the realms" (S2.21) — and he speculates that the Crownspire gate may be the very anchor by which others "have sealed the Tir'van away." This is why [[mags]] catches his uncharacteristic flash of excitement at the tampered gate (S2.22) — it is the lead he has been hunting all his life.

Morally, Rell is the party's harshest realist. He is bitter that the party "dance around the problem" out of nicety while villagers die, that they trust the [[the-eldryn]] far too readily ("Enough rope to hang ourselves by"), and that they mistake his fellows' fearlessness for bravery rather than "arrogance and ignorance." Yet the forest's brutal honesty moves him, and he comes to value [[fabian-perennius]] (whom he gifts a wordless communion on Nameweave) and grudgingly [[caspian-talon]] (whose curiosity he finds "refreshing"). The poverty of his people drives his temptation toward the [[the-dragon-bones]] — "a single bone might see my family out of the slums."

## DM notes / secrets
- **The "wizard" identity is a guise.** Rell is officially known as a wizard and deliberately never names a subclass. In truth he is an **Aberrant Mind sorcerer** (an "aberrant soul" sorcerer), masking his innate, aberrant power behind the studied respectability of wizardry — a concealment that fits the unsettling, many-eyed nature of the [[the-tir-van]].
- **Rell's excitement at the gate (S2.22) is a seeded suspicion**: his personal quest for the [[the-horizon-doors]] could put him at cross-purposes with the [[the-eldryn]]'s goal of sealing crossings to stop the Uthrel. He may want a gate *opened*. His solo **unicorn theory** of the gate-tampering (S2.28) is of a piece with this fixation — he reads the crossing more closely than anyone else does.
- He refuses oaths (notably lying, like Caspian, when the Eldryn demand the vow, S2.18) — a standing trust issue.
- The Tir'Van elders' lore (the sealed gate, "Aerek" who may have known how to open them) is Rell's private research thread.
- **The "self-created spell" cover (S2.29):** Rell uses a long-lasting telepathic link — connecting up to **three** people to him at once, reaching about a **mile**, lasting roughly an hour, and working as a **relay** (each linked person speaks only with Rell, not a group chat); a separate, shorter casting earlier in the day gave a five-hour one-to-one link with Caspian. He **describes it as an original spell he honed and created on the road**, but this is part of his **wizard disguise** — in truth it is his innate (Aberrant Mind) sorcery, dressed up as studied wizardry. Treat the "self-created" framing as Rell's in-character claim, not fact.
