## Overview
Hesk is a centaur leader of the hidden [[the-eldryn-camp]] — a wary druid who, with her captain [[ethis]], commands the temporary mobilisation of centaurs and firbolgs containing the forest crisis (S2.18). She is the gatekeeper of the [[the-eldryn]]'s great secret and, by S2.21, a confirmed member of the Eldryn **Council**.

## Knowledge & motivations
Hesk's driving concern is containment of [[the-uthrel]] and protection of the wider world from it — even at the cost of starving her own forest and lake. She initially binds the party by oath not to leave the camp or investigate, refusing to explain anything, and reluctantly agrees (after [[fabian-perennius]]'s argument) only to send word to the town to keep worse outsiders away (S2.18). She is proud and guarded — Rell reads superiority and pride — but visibly **saddened** by what the Eldryn are doing.

Won over by the party's honesty (and [[mags]]'s sincere Zone of Truth at Nameweave), she relents and tells the truth: the moss is the uthrel, a Feywild scourge that drove out the elves; the Eldryn contained an outbreak by burning the forest and warding off all ambient magic, which is why the [[the-quickened|Quickened]] turned hostile (S2.21). She teaches the party how to destroy uthrel, releases them from their vow, and allies with them. When the [[the-bright-gate]]'s wards prove deliberately tampered with by an outsider, she — pale and shaken — agrees other crossings must be checked and convenes the Eldryn Great Council early (S2.22).

## Appearances
- S2.18: Leads the camp; extracts the party's oath; agrees to warn the town.
- S2.19–20: Presides as the party covertly investigates; breaks up the Nameweave gathering when "uthrel" is spoken.
- S2.21: Reveals the truth of the uthrel; teaches its destruction; allies with the party.
- S2.22: Convinced the gate was tampered with by a stranger; convenes the Council early and posts a watch.
- S2.24: Referenced as the party returns; her Council acts on the gate intelligence.

## DM notes / secrets
- Hesk's faction debated in "parliament" whether to burn the forest; some argue her containment perimeter is too large and is needlessly starving the Quickened (S2.20) — a live political fracture in the Eldryn that the party glimpsed and could exploit.
