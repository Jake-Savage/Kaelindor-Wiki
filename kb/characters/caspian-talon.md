## Overview
Caspian Talon is a human Divination Wizard and one of the three founding members of the party (with [[dakir]] and [[trevick]]), meeting them in [[stonehaven]] for [[lady-aldara]]'s [[nerrin]] job (S1.01). He is the party's scholar and theorist: he researches threats before engaging them, keeps obsessive field notes, and values facts over the "gods and spirits" he openly disdains. His rat familiar [[theodore]] serves as an invaluable scout. He carries [[caspians-portable-library]] and an array of utility magic, and has connections to the [[order-of-the-tower-of-heaven]] through High Mage [[shalfay]].

## Knowledge & motivations
Caspian is driven by an almost amoral hunger to understand. His diaries reveal a man who would gladly build a "small pocket dimension" lab to experiment on a live [[the-uthrel]] sample, and who is genuinely anguished that the [[the-gardener|Gardener]] destroyed his only specimen (S1.04) — "you need to understand a thing to eradicate it" (S2.20). The young silver-dragon bones in [[the-dragon-grove]] nearly break him: he cannot believe he was forced to leave "an entire dragon skeleton" unstudied, calling such a find priceless to "the educated world," and conspires with [[fabian-perennius]] to relocate and exploit them (S2.16). He maps the route back so the grove can be found again.

He is privately scornful of religion and rustic life, was raised somewhere "backwater" (Tomin's Field is named in others' diaries as Fabian's, not his — Caspian's origin remains unstated), and was once trained by a harsh woman ("She drummed that sort of nonsense out of me") who taught him to distrust reliance on innate magic — a lesson he applies to diagnosing the tampered wards at [[the-bright-gate]] (S2.22). His curiosity about the [[the-horizon-doors|gate]] tampering, the thinning [[the-feywild-barrier]], and the "wrong" prophetic books at the Tower drives the late-chapter investigation.

## DM notes / secrets
- Caspian's solo dealings with [[lucien]] are a deliberate hole in the party's knowledge. He went alone to a powerful vampire and came back shaken, and his "book made me resistant to charms" explanation does not convince [[mags]]. The ingest flags a **possible undead affliction or hidden compromise** acquired from Lucien — he is "evasive about possible undead affliction." This is unresolved and worth keeping ambiguous.
- He retains [[kiona-dumois|Kiona]]'s [[codex-mortis]] notes (dangerous necromantic knowledge that reveals secrets over time).
- His secret map back to the silver-dragon grove (S2.16) is a future flashpoint with [[dakir]] and the [[the-eldryn]].
