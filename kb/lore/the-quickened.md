## What it is
The Quickened are place-bound nature spirits — the living spirits of the lake, forest, and land. They cannot be spoken to directly, only interpreted by druids who read their behaviour (e.g. watching otters dance). Together with the [[the-eldryn|Eldryn]] they form the [[the-prior]] — the peoples who held these lands before the [[the-valenwyr|Valenwyr]] settled.

## What's known
- The lake spirits near [[greymere|Greymere]] are the **Yarrows**. They turned hostile and killed fishermen, which is what drew the party to Greymere (the Greymere crisis).
- Their hostility is a **symptom of magic-starvation**, not malice: the [[the-eldryn|Eldryn]] burned part of the [[the-eldryn-forest|forest]] and warded off all ambient magic to contain the [[the-uthrel|uthrel]], which cut the Quickened off from the magic they need to survive (S2.20-21). Druid [[anwen-ireth|Anwen Ireth]], who communes with them, hinted the cause lay west (S2.13).
- The [[illyra-fen|hedge-witch's]] wild-magic charm may have drawn the Yarrows to one victim — magic attracts them (Reflections, Dakir entry 14).
- The [[the-driftcrown|driftcrown]] is a rare bloom-Quickened, an omen of fortune. [[trevick|Trevick]] tried to send one back to the Yarrows as a gesture of goodwill (Reflections, Trevick entry 12).
- Crossing the [[the-quickened-boundary|Quickened boundary]] (the Thal Stones) is treated as a natural death.

## Open questions
- Do the Quickened think and reason as people do, or act purely on instinct? [[dakir|Dakir]] and [[rell-aetris|Rell]] debate exactly this — and whether retaliating against instinct with logic is even just (Reflections, Dakir entry 13; Rell entry 13).
- Will restoring the magic balance (once the uthrel is dealt with) return them to their old behaviour?
