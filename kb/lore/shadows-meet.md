## What it is
Shadows Meet, also called **the Vigil**, is a festival of [[itharis|Itharis]] held on the **1st of Ithar's Veil** (roughly November). It runs mainly from dusk onwards and continues through the night. Participants **wear masks**. It is one of the very few Itharian rites that are **not secret** — most observances of the [[the-shadowed-path|Shadowed Path]] are hidden, but Shadows Meet happens openly enough that outsiders will have seen it, even if they do not take part.

## What's known
DM-established (S2.28, expanded S2.29):

- **Date and form:** 1 Ithar's Veil; a dusk-to-dawn, masked observance.
- **Who participates:** an Itharian rite, but **anyone may join in** the festivities — even those devoted to other gods will take part if they are in a place where it is happening, since the gods are not exclusive. Non-Itharians do not perform the deeper rites (unlike the broadly-shared [[nameweave|Nameweave]]), but the public celebration is open and outsiders join freely.
- **Pilgrimage:** it draws heavy pilgrimage to [[greywatch]] in [[the-mirrowen|the Mirrowen]], which the Itharians hold sacred as a place of thresholds and crossings. Pilgrims arrive via the Drake's Road (many flying in to Drake Mount first), overcrowding the town; hawkers sell festival masks on the approach.
- **Form of the night (S2.29):** events happen at **different, unannounced places** around town — nobody declares beforehand where things will be; revellers wander and stumble across them. The custom is a "delicate dance" of poking around for things "interesting enough but not too interesting" and staying in one's comfort zone; people are well aware that much happens during Shadows Meet they have no wish to witness.
- **Festival brews (S2.29):** special drinks, foods and places mark the night. Among the gnomes of Greywatch, a potent perception-altering spirit colloquially called **"the bad idea"** is brewed each year specifically for the festival — strongly alcoholic and licorice-scented, it induces a liminal, mildly hallucinatory state of awareness. [[fabian-perennius]] recalls (History) that such perception-altering brews are **typical of Itharian rites**, meant to put participants into a threshold state of awareness. Some gnomes honour the festival simply by sitting together and drinking it all night.
- **Context:** the party arrives in Greywatch on 28 Dusklin 1568, a few days ahead of the festival, and buys masks (see [[ch02-s28]]); on S2.29 they decide to **leave town before the festival** to pursue [[kethons-fold|Kethon's Fold]] (see [[the-korrathian-conspiracy]]). The DM noted the festival's timing relative to the party is coincidence, not plot.
