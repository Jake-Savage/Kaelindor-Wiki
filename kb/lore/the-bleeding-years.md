## What it is
The Bleeding Years are the present, grinding war between the western [[the-valenwyr|Valenwyr]] nations and the elvish empire of **Thal'Mirrith**. It is "magic, for the most part, that has continued to hold the line" — mages spend the wealth of nations and their own lives maintaining wards against the dark elvish magic that constantly probes the defences.

## What's known
- **Origin.** About 120 years ago, the young, militaristic Archon **Thaloriel** came to power in the elvish nation of **Mirrithiel** (in the great north-eastern forest the elves had settled and renamed the **Elenfain**). He closed the borders, secretly built up his strength for three decades, then renamed his kingdom **Thal'Mirrith** and fell on the Valenwyr nations to the south, conquering them swiftly.
- **Dark magic.** The elves wielded dark magics "not seen before," leading some to conclude Thaloriel "had made a deal with some unholy power."
- **The line holds.** ~90 years ago the kingdoms west of the **Stone March** (a north-south mountain range with few crossings) formed the **Erythian League**, led by [[erythar|Erythar]]. The passes were held in a series of bloody battles, and the war settled into the present attritional stalemate.
- **The cost.** Resources are stretched everywhere; the best of everything is sent to [[crownspire|Crownspire]] for the war effort. Poorer kingdoms pay their tithe in fighters and mages for the front line. [[kaelindor|Kaelindor]] is wealthy and far from the front, paying its tithe in gold and magical items rather than soldiers.
- **Dissident elves.** Not all elves support Thaloriel; some live in exile in the western lands, welcomed by some kingdoms and forbidden in others.
