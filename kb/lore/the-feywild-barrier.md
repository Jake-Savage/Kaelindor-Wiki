## What it is
The Feywild barrier is the planar boundary separating the Material Plane from the [[the-feywild|Feywild]]. It is not perfectly solid: it can thin, and crossings can open. The party's investigation into the [[the-uthrel|uthrel]]'s source repeatedly turned on the state of this barrier and the points where it can be breached.

## What's known
- Ancient **gates** anchor and strengthen the barrier. The [[the-bright-gate|Bright Gate]] (Thalasyr Eren) is one such crossing; the Eldryn deliberately did **not** destroy it because the gate anchors the wards that hold the planar barrier closed (S2.22).
- Crossings can be deliberately **tampered with**: someone altered the Bright Gate's wards to pass through and then restored them, hoping no one would notice (S2.22). [[caspian-talon|Caspian]] concluded "something has come from the feywild, bringing the Uthrel with it" but that the intruder "knew to cover their tracks" (Reflections, Caspian entry 21).
- [[the-faerie-ring|Faerie rings]] are minor, unpredictable crossing-points; casting magic into one in the Eldryn forest summoned a mist elemental and petrified [[mags|Mags]] (S1.16 / Reflections).
- The barrier's weakness, [[caspian-talon|Caspian]] argues, comes from over-reliance on innate magic the Eldryn "do not truly understand," which let the wards be tampered with (Reflections, Caspian entry 21).

## Open questions
- Who tampered with the Bright Gate, and how did they obtain magic "foreign to the Eldryn"? (See [[korrathian]], [[the-furnace-brood]].)
- How many crossings exist, and are others compromised? Hesk resolved to check the other crossings (S2.22).
- Is the barrier related to the [[the-horizon-doors|Horizon Doors]], which access other worlds entirely? Rell wonders if the uthrel's breach could herald a Horizon gate (Reflections, Rell entry 20).
