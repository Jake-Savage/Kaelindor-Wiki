## What it is
"Korrathian" refers to the people, language, and magic of **Korrath** — an infernal culture. The Korrathian language is Infernal; Korrathian magic is granted by infernal pact. The [[the-furnace-brood|Furnace Brood]] is a brutal Korrathian faction whose agent [[hethan-kroll|Hethan Kroll]] was sent to probe the [[the-uthrel|uthrel]] incursion. They are a suspect in the wider conspiracy, but are **not** established as the gate-tamperer — and per [[mags|Mags]]'s Speak with Dead, the Brood themselves do not know who caused the incursion.

## What's known
- **Hethan Kroll's contract.** The mercenary captain [[hethan-kroll|Hethan Kroll]] — magically untalented — was granted Korrathian magic and pay by the Furnace Brood's "Broodfather" via a Korrathian (Infernal) "contract," paid through a Crownspire bank (S2.24).
- **The gate tampering — tamperer unknown.** The [[the-bright-gate|Bright Gate]]'s wards were altered by a "stranger" whose magic is "foreign to the Eldryn" (S2.22); their identity is unknown. Whether a Korrathian hand did it is **unconfirmed** — Kroll was sent only to check "if the incursion left a trace" (S2.24), and the Brood's own ignorance of the cause argues against them being the tamperer.
- **A Bridgemarch cell.** Two long-resident tieflings (resident ~9 years, posing as Korrath refugees) ran a warded metalworking shop in [[bridgemarch|Bridgemarch]] as a Korrathian contact point; they kept a repeatedly-used **blood-and-ash shrine to the Furnace Brood** and killed themselves rather than be taken (S2.26).
- **Concealment magic** is a hallmark of how the Korrathians operate on the Material Plane (S2.26).

## Open questions
- What is the **true purpose** of the incursion through the gate, and who actually caused it? Even Kroll did not know (S2.24); the cell's suicide left it unresolved (the chapter cliffhanger).
- How does Korrath relate to the [[the-bleeding-years|war]] and the elves' "unholy power," or to the [[the-ruin-of-vraxyn|Ruin of Vraxyn]] (fire/unmaking)?
- What does [[oscar-wicklow|Chancellor Wicklow]] know — he lied about the uthrel and kept Kroll's map (S2.26).
