## What it is
A Watcher is a concentrated spirit that [[rhynharran|Rhynharran]] sends to watch over those who must witness or do something important. The firbolgs of the [[the-eldryn|Eldryn]] revere it. For [[trevick|Trevick]], the Watcher takes the form of his spirit companion [[hawk]] — which the firbolgs immediately recognised as no ordinary bird (S2.19).

## What's known
- When Trevick summoned Hawk before the firbolgs, they reacted with **reverence**, naming it a "Watcher" — a spirit of Rhynharran (S2.19).
- The Watcher has watched **Trevick's family across generations**; the firbolgs say this is both an **honour and a burden** (S2.19).
- The firbolg [[he-who-rests-where-the-path-narrows]] explained the Watcher to Trevick. Trevick had earlier half-heard the words "Watcher.... Guide...." and found them strange (Reflections, Trevick entry 14).
- Hesk weighs the significance of Trevick's Watcher as the party earns the Eldryn's trust (S2.21; quest [[trevicks-watcher]]).

## Open questions

- Is the Watcher connected to Trevick's unknown father (a "Salmeros," known to the devil [[joster-sirit|Joster]]) and his destroyed village?
- Does Hawk grow in power or awareness as Trevick's role unfolds? (Trevick "begins to sense Hawk is no ordinary bird.")
