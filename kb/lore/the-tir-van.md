## What it is
The Tir'Van, also called the **Horizonborn**, are [[rell-aetris|Rell]]'s people: a reclusive, masked folk with **many-eyed faces** who communicate not in words but by **direct, wordless communion of thought** — sharing meaning mind-to-mind in a way language cannot dilute or warp. Among themselves there is no lying; "trust, implicit and entire, is as natural to us as the breath we draw" (Reflections, Rell entry 18).

## What's known
- They came to Valenya through a [[the-horizon-doors|Horizon Door]] from a homeland the elders speak of only reluctantly — "only that it was a battle for survival" (Reflections, Rell entry 20).
- Rell keeps his face **masked** in the wider world; he unmasked and communed wordlessly with [[fabian-perennius|Fabian]] as a Nameweave gift, an experience Fabian found uniquely beautiful (S2.20; Reflections, Fabian/Rell entries 19).
- Because they already share thought directly, [[nameweave|Nameweave]] (a [[naelos|Naelos]] festival of community and transparency) is "almost redundant" for them — but they value it as a day when prejudice against them eases, since "prejudice on Nameweave is surely heresy" (Reflections, Rell entry 18).
- Rell's family was glimpsed at Nameweave with a warmth and closeness that struck Fabian (Reflections, Fabian entry 19).

## Open questions
- **Were the Tir'Van sealed away?** Rell fears the people of [[crownspire|Crownspire]] used the Crownspire Horizon Door to **seal his homeland shut**, leaving those left behind to suffer (Reflections, Rell entry 21).
- **Can the gates be reopened?** Rell believes an elder, "Aerek," may have known how to open the gates and "unwind these seals," and he means to get word home (Reflections, Rell entry 21).
- **Is the [[the-uthrel|uthrel]] connected to the Tir'Van homeland?** Rell wonders if it is "corruption from the homelands" and whether following it to its source will lead him to his people (Reflections, Rell entry 20).
- Why does Rell refuse oaths and react with suspicious excitement to the tampered gate? (S2.20, S2.22.)
