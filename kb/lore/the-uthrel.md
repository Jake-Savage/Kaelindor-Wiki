## What it is
The uthrel is the campaign's central mystery: a creeping, magic-devouring growth that looks like black moss but is not moss, and is not truly alive. It comes from the Feywild, is dangerous to all living things, and is feared by the [[the-eldryn|Eldryn]] above almost anything else. Where it takes hold it consumes ambient magic, withers the life and spirit out of a place, and leaves behind a magic-starved ruin. Rell, of the [[the-tir-van|Tir'Van]], calls it an "arcanophage" — a thing that eats magic (Reflections, Rell entry 20).

The party encountered it long before they had a name for it. They first met it as "the moss" in the [[nerrin-mine]] (S1.01-04), where it grew alongside a [[meenlocks|meenlock]] nest. [[trevick|Trevick]]'s home village of Velton was destroyed by it roughly three years ago — he returned from a hunt to find the village ransacked, his mother gone, and the moss (which had been present when he left) vanished, the village ageing years in days (Reflections, Trevick entry 19). It surfaced again in the dying [[the-eldryn-forest|Eldryn forest]] around [[greymere|Greymere]], and was finally named by the Eldryn in S2.20.

## What's known
- **Name and nature.** The Eldryn call it the **uthrel**. When [[trevick|Trevick]] described the "black moss" to the firbolgs at Nameweave, the whole camp erupted in alarm (S2.19-20). [[hesk|Hesk]] later confirmed its nature: not a moss, not alive, hard to detect, and dangerous to everything that lives (S2.21).
- **Origin in the Feywild.** It entered the [[the-feywild|Feywild]] roughly **900 years ago** and is only now returning to the Material Plane (S2.21). Both the Feywild and the underdark are hyper-fertile places where it grows easily — a point [[shalfay|Shalfay]]'s prophetic books also returned as a "warning against contamination" (S1.04).
- **It drove out the elves.** The uthrel is the reason the elves fled their Feywild homeland; the Eldryn fear it is being eyed as a weapon in the great eastern war ([[the-bleeding-years]]) (S2.21).
- **How it spreads.** It crosses to the Material Plane carried by travellers over planar crossings. Travel to the Feywild is no longer possible (S2.21). The Eldryn traced one outbreak to the [[the-bright-gate|Bright Gate]] (Thalasyr Eren), an ancient sealed Feywild crossing in the burnt [[the-hallow-burn|Hallow Burn]] — and found its wards had been deliberately tampered with so an outsider could pass through and re-seal them (S2.22).
- **How to destroy it.** The Eldryn taught the party the method (S2.21): isolate it with **anti-magic wards** to cut off ambient magic; destroy the uthrel itself with **magical fire**; burn its physical fuel with **mundane fire**; avoid the **gas** it releases; then check for spread. The Eldryn contained their own outbreak by burning a ~1km zone of forest and warding off all ambient magic — which is why the forest, the lake, and the [[the-quickened|Quickened]] are starving (S2.20-21).
- **A symbol of taboo.** The deep gnome [[eklash|Eklash]] recognised the moss as taboo in the early arc, and Mags's gnomish nursery rhyme warns children that "Uthrel walks the underways / And waits for you beneath" (Reflections, Mags entry 19) — suggesting the underdark gnomes have feared it for generations under the same name.
- **The Feywild fears it too.** When shown a sample, the unicorns [[stargleam]] and [[silverlily]] were horrified and [[the-gardener|the Gardener]] (an Archfey) disintegrated it outright, refusing to discuss it (S1.04).

## Open questions
- **How did it enter the Feywild in the first place?** ~900 years ago is established, but the source is unknown to everyone the party has met (S2.21).
- **Who tampered with the [[the-bright-gate|Bright Gate]], and why?** The trail leads to [[hethan-kroll|Hethan Kroll]], the [[the-furnace-brood|Furnace Brood]], and a [[korrathian|Korrathian]] conspiracy — but Kroll himself said his job was only to check "if the incursion left a trace," and the incursion's true purpose is unknown even to him (S2.24). The Bridgemarch cell suicided rather than be taken (S2.26).
- **What is [[oscar-wicklow|Chancellor Wicklow]] hiding?** Mags and Caspian caught him lying when he denied knowing of the uthrel (S2.26).
- **Is the uthrel connected to Rell's people?** Rell wonders whether it is "some corruption from the homelands" beyond a [[the-horizon-doors|Horizon Door]], and whether it could be the cause of the [[the-tir-van|Tir'Van]] exodus (Reflections, Rell entry 20).
- **Is there a link to the [[the-disappearance-of-dragons|disappearance of the dragons]] or the elves' war?** Both happened in roughly overlapping windows; the party suspects "there is more to the war than we know" (S2.20).
- **Can it be studied without spreading it?** [[caspian-talon|Caspian]] is desperate to obtain a sample for research (his was destroyed by the Gardener), and the Eldryn rightly fear anyone who wants to "gain something" from it — notoriety, a weapon, or worse (Reflections, Caspian entry 20).
