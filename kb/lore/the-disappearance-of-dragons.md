## What it is
Roughly 300 years ago, dragons — which had never been common — began to disappear, gradually, over several decades, until they were all gone. No one has seen a living dragon in centuries. This was one of several great changes that marked Valenya entering its "current technological era" (alongside the elves settling the Elenfain and the [[calvyr|Calvyr]] priests' [[constructs|constructs]], airships, and compasses).

## What's known
- Wyverns still exist, especially in the mountains, and are sometimes loosely called "dragons" — but they are smaller, non-sentient animals, not the true dragons that vanished.
- **Dragon remains and hoards** are occasionally discovered, but a full skeleton is extraordinarily rare. [[caspian-talon|Caspian]] notes decades pass between dragon discoveries (Reflections, Caspian entry 15).
- The party found a near-complete **silver dragon skeleton** in a sacred grove in the Eldryn forest ([[the-dragon-grove]], S2.15). [[dakir|Dakir]] felt his bronze draconic magic resonate with the bones — "different yet familiar" — and secretly took some (the [[the-dragon-bones]]); [[fabian-perennius|Fabian]] dreamed of the Crownspire Museum; the Eldryn guard the grove as sacred (Reflections, multiple entries 15).
- The first dragon remains returned to civilisation after the disappearance came from "Aelorin Vaelthas' Stonemarch Expedition" (Fabian, Reflections entry 15).

## Open questions
- **Why did the dragons vanish?** No in-world explanation is established. [[dakir|Dakir]] explicitly asks whether the bones could tell him "more about what happened to the dragons" and whether any remain alive (Reflections, Dakir entry 15).
- Is the disappearance connected to the source of Dakir's own draconic magic (quest [[dakirs-draconic-heritage]])?
- Does it relate in time to the elves' war or the [[the-uthrel|uthrel]] entering the Feywild ~900 years ago? (Timelines overlap loosely.)
