## What it is
"The Prior" is the collective name for those who were in the land before the settling [[the-valenwyr|Valenwyr]] arrived: the [[the-eldryn|Eldryn]] (centaurs, goliaths, firbolgs) and the [[the-quickened|Quickened]] (place-bound nature spirits). The setting summary notes the Valenwyr "are not the first civilisation to settle these lands," and that species such as firbolgs, goliaths and centaurs are "thought to be indigenous to the land" and not counted among the Valenwyr.

## What's known
- The Prior occupy and guard the wild places — the deep forests, the lakes, the mountains. The Eldryn guard the Branweald and contain the [[the-uthrel|uthrel]].
- Disturbing the Prior "at scale" is what [[rell-aetris|Rell]] suspected was behind the Greymere crisis (Reflections, Rell entry 12) — and he was essentially right: the uthrel and the Eldryn's containment effort had upset the balance the Prior maintain.
- Their ways are alien to the Valenwyr: long-lived, patient, bound by oath and ritual, and deeply distrustful of outsiders carrying forest secrets (S2.17-19).

## Open questions
- How much living memory do the long-lived Eldryn hold that the people of Greymere have forgotten? Trevick suspects the [[the-irdane|Irdane]] role and other duties lapsed across the short generations of the Valenwyr (Reflections, Trevick entry 17).
