## Overview
Rhynharran is the god of **nature, weather, the wild, and its cycles** — the stag, the storm, the green and growing land. Worship is organised through [[the-warding-of-rhynharran|Rhynharran's Ward]], commonly "the Warding," whose houses adapt to local conditions: **Greenhalls, Stonehalls, Stormhalls, and Seahalls** (collectively Ward Halls).

## What's known
- **Clerical hierarchy:** Wildtender (novices/layfolk tending groves, animals, sacred fires); Hornbearers (initiates who call gatherings with the stag's horn); **Greenhearts** (priests named for their place — Greenheart, Stoneheart, Stormheart, Seaheart — who preside over local halls); **[[the-weathered|The Weathered]]** (solitary pilgrim-hermits, often druids); and **The Oak Heart**, the highest figure, the living embodiment of Rhynharran's strength and guardianship (Churches of Kaelindor).
- At [[greymere|Greymere]], the Warding's hall ([[rhynharrans-hall]]) is led by the Greenheart [[thalos-briarwood|Thalos Briarwood]], who counsels patience. The [[the-weathered|Weathered]] [[reina|Reina]] and [[elset|Elset]] wander the forest as solitary pilgrims.
- Rhynharran sends **[[the-watcher|Watchers]]** — concentrated spirits that watch over a chosen lineage across generations. [[trevick|Trevick]]'s spirit [[hawk]] is one such Watcher (S2.19).
- [[serris-dane|Serris Dane]] is a fervent Rhynharran devotee who believes recreating the storm of [[the-stormblind|the Stormblind]] will let his god take him up.
- Trevick finds the **firbolg interpretation** of Rhynharran (via [[nessa|Nessa]]) resonates with him far more than the formal Greenhall worship (Reflections, Trevick entry 12).
