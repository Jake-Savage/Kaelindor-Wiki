## Overview

The Church of Vraxyn — proper name **The Dance of the Burning Blade**, called **The Unmaking** among devotees but more often **The Ruin** among ordinary folk — venerates [[vraxyn]] through fire, destruction, and the ecstatic joy of unmaking. It is treated as a cult rather than a respectable faith; its rites involve offerings of fire or blood and dances of blade and flame. Its houses of worship are the **Queen's Chantries**, known commonly as **Chantrys** or, less flatteringly, **Burnhouses**.

The Ruin's footprint in the campaign overlaps with the [[the-furnace-brood|Furnace Brood]] and the Korrathian conspiracy: a hidden blood-and-ash shrine and ritual murders bear the cult's hallmarks of fire and unmaking.

## Structure / ranks

Worship houses: **Chantrys** / **Burnhouses** (formally *the Queen's Chantry*).

Clerical ladder, lowest to highest:
- **Cinderborn** — novices, newly dedicated through offerings of fire or blood.
- **Ashdancers** — initiates who practice the ecstatic dances of blade and flame.
- **Flamebearers** — priests who tend Burnhouses and lead rites of fire and destruction.
- **Fireblades** — high clergy who preside over the Queen's Chantries, feared for unleashing Vraxyn's joy in unmaking.
- **The Ash Lord** — the supreme figure of the cult, embodying the Ash Queen's consort; whether a single person or a shifting mantle is uncertain.

## Relationship to the party

- **S2.24:** Opening [[hethan-kroll]]'s box, the party found a [[the-furnace-brood|Furnace Brood]] sigil and a Korrathian contract; the Brood's fire-and-unmaking ethos ties it to the worship of Vraxyn. (See [[krolls-korrathian-contract]].)
- **S2.26:** [[captain-thoronby]]'s raid on the Bridgemarch [[the-tiefling-shop|metalworking shop]] uncovered a **blood-and-ash shrine to the Furnace Brood**, ritually used many times — a hallmark of Vraxyn's cult — guarded by a shadow-tentacle illusion. The resident tieflings killed themselves before capture.

The party has had no direct dealings with the Ruin's clergy; the cult's presence is inferred from Korrathian/Furnace Brood shrines and ritual killings rather than open contact, and its full role in the conspiracy is unresolved.
