## Overview

The Church of Rhynharran — proper name **Rhynharran's Ward**, commonly **The Warding** — venerates [[rhynharran]] as the god of nature, weather, beasts, and the wild's cycles. Its clergy are priests and druids who tend groves, animals, and sacred fires, and who guide their communities through the turning of the seasons. Its houses of worship — **Greenhalls, Stonehalls, Stormhalls, or Seahalls** depending on local conditions — are known commonly as **Ward Halls**.

The Warding is central to the [[greymere]] arc: its hall there, under the Greenheart [[thalos-briarwood]], commissioned the party's forest mission, and the church's spirit-emissary the [[the-watcher|Watcher]] is bound to [[trevick]]'s bloodline through his spirit hawk [[hawk]].

## Structure / ranks

Worship houses: **Ward Halls** (Greenhalls / Stonehalls / Stormhalls / Seahalls by local conditions).

Clerical ladder, lowest to highest:
- **Wildtender** — novices and layfolk who tend groves, animals, and sacred fires.
- **Hornbearers** — initiates who call gatherings with the stag's horn and assist in rites.
- **Greenhearts** — priests named for their place (Greenheart, Stoneheart, Stormheart, Seaheart) who preside over local halls and guide their communities through nature's cycles.
- **The Weathered** — pilgrims who have spent long years as solitary wilderness hermits, often (not always) druids. (See [[the-weathered]].)
- **The Oak Heart** — the highest figure of the Warding, revered as the living embodiment of Rhynharran's strength and guardianship.

He counselled patience, showed [[dakir]] no distaste, recounted [[the-stormblind|the Stormblind]] legend, and admitted his messengers to the [[the-eldryn|Eldryn]] had not returned. - **S2.12–S2.13:** Thalos again counselled patience and hinted the cause lay far west, beneath the lake; Anwen Ireth confirmed the larger Quickened dwell there. [[trevick]] left a respectful written forest offering.
- **S2.14:** The devotee [[serris-dane]] had grown estranged from the Hall, believing his prophetic dreams told him more than the priests knew.
- **S2.15:** **The Greenheart commissioned [[trevick]]** to find the Eldryn and learn the fate of the three missing messengers, including the [[the-weathered|Weathered]] druid [[reina]].
- **S2.19:** Firbolgs revealed [[hawk]] is a **[[the-watcher|Watcher]]** — a spirit Rhynharran sends to watch an important lineage across generations, watching Trevick's family.
- **S2.23–S2.24:** Centaurs escorted the party back to the Hall, where the displaced [[nessa]] and the recovered Weathered [[reina]] and [[elset]] had sheltered. Briarwood confirmed the messenger [[vasha]]'s movements; Mags performed Speak with Dead on Kroll's body there. The party reconciled [[mathis-irdane]] and [[serris-dane]] as joint [[the-irdane|Irdane]].

The Warding is the party's primary patron in the Greymere crisis and the spiritual frame for Trevick's Watcher.
