## Overview

The Church of Itharis — proper name **The Shadowed Path**, commonly **The Path** — venerates [[itharis]] as the god of death, endings, transformation, and the great transitions between one state and the next. Its clergy attend the dying, conduct death rites, and officiate the largest changes a person or society can undergo. Its houses of worship are the **Halls of Silver Light**, commonly called **Shadowhouses**.

The Path is the most secretive of the five churches, and its presence in the campaign is felt indirectly — through a suspected hidden priest in high office and through a killing that bore the marks of an Itharian death-ritual.

## Structure / ranks

Worship houses: **Shadowhouses** (formally *Halls of Silver Light*).

Clerical ladder, lowest to highest:
- **Seekers** — novices who tend the shadowhouses and offer comfort and counsel to those who need a place to contemplate.
- **Walkers-in-Darkness** — those dedicated to the church, assisting in matters of practical change and loss, attending the dying, and conducting death rites.
- **Walkers-in-Shadow** — initiates of the higher mysteries; they lead rituals of transformation and change, including symbolic death and rebirth.
- **Stranger** — feared and revered figures who embody Itharis's mysteries, officiating the greatest transitions: coronations, exiles, the founding or dissolution of noble households. Some say their touch alone can sever the bonds of a former life.
- **The Passing Shadow** — appointed only when diviners indicate a great, world-altering change is possible. The last known Passing Shadow served 300 years ago, during the founding of the Elenfain.

## Relationship to the party

- **S2.10:** Chancellor [[oscar-wicklow]] — **rumoured to be a secret priest of Itharis** — personally briefed the party on the [[greymere]] job, offering official favour plus 1400 gold.
- **S2.26:** Mags and Caspian caught Wicklow **lying** when he denied any knowledge of the [[the-uthrel|uthrel]], and he took keen interest in [[hethan-kroll]]'s marked map. Kroll's killing — a single clean blow, surroundings untouched — bore the **marks of an Itharian crossing-ritual**, hinting at active Path interest in containing the Korrathian conspiracy.

The party has had no overt dealings with the Church itself; its hand is suspected behind Wicklow's manoeuvring and the ritual nature of Kroll's death.
