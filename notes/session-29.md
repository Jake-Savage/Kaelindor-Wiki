# Session 29 - 30/06/2026

29 Dusklin, 1568

The party has settled into discreet lodging in Greywatch, the contact's address in hand. It spends
a full day working that address, splitting into two groups: Mags, Fabian and Trevick gather gossip
in the streets, while Caspian and Rell stake out the address itself. The town remains crowded ahead
of Shadows Meet, the mood broadly positive.

## Greywatch streets — Mags, Fabian & Trevick

Mags moves through the crowds offering small help and drawing out conversation, steering it toward
local powers and, eventually, the address. Mags sets aside her visible signs of being a priest of
Naelos to pass unremarked. The party learns the following:

- The Mirrowen as a whole is ruled through Greywatch by an official titled the Warden of the Beacon
  (also Beacon Keeper), elected from the town council for a twelve-year term.
- The current Warden, Ivo Pierron ("Sir Ivo"), is in the last year of his term, with about eight
  months left, and the town speculates about his successor.
- The successor must be a sitting council member (the council numbers fourteen; only two or three
  are credible candidates) and must hold the approval of Trevaryn's ruler, Lord Valcarin, to whom
  the outgoing Warden is close. Greywatch's council is not family-based; seats roughly represent the
  parts of the Mirrowen that happen to be habitable at a given time.
- Trevaryn, by contrast, is ruled by eight leading families who elect a ruler for a twenty-five-year
  term — a remnant of an older clan system.
- Townsfolk assume the Itharian Church effectively controls the council, but there is no formal
  relationship. The common view is that the Mirrowen has "two powers," the Itharian Church and Drake
  Mount, and no one knows how far they act together, because the Itharians are so secretive.

Dropped into conversation, the address is readily recognised. It is the Three Copper Hatch — properly
"Potter's Pots" — a hole-in-the-wall food business run by Jorrith Potter from a hatch in his house in
a poor quarter, the new district built over the old dragon-landing ground. Pressed further, the
gossip about Jorrith runs:

- He is a human man in his fifties, lives alone, and is well-liked but pitied. He makes remarkably
  cheap, honest food and takes a genuine interest in his regulars.
- He grew up in Greywatch, left for about a decade (going somewhere in Trevaryn, exact place
  unknown), and returned with his wife to set up the business.
- His wife, Meera, was the cheerful, sociable driving force behind it; he was always the quieter,
  wearier of the two. They had no children. She died of a quick, unidentified illness after about
  three years back in town, and he has carried on alone and joylessly since.
- Townsfolk disagree on whether Meera was a tiefling — she had "a touch of soot about her" but no
  obvious features — which the DM frames as consistent with distant, later-generation tiefling
  descent, where planar traits fade after a few generations away from Karath.
- The Mirrowen does not bury its dead — owing to past incidents of bodies not staying buried — so
  Meera was cremated.

## The hatch — Caspian & Rell

Concurrently, Caspian and Rell stake out the hatch. The streets here are narrow and crowded, the
houses small and mismatched.

- Rell walks the street under Detect Thoughts, probing for a concealment field like the one that
  blanketed the Bridgemarch shop. He finds nothing as powerful or blunt as that, but cannot rule
  out something smaller and subtler; the result is inconclusive.
- Rell then moves rooftop-to-rooftop to a discreet vantage and, with one of his features, opens a
  long telepathic link to Caspian.
- Caspian casts Invisibility and watches the house. Jorrith is plainly cooking through the morning,
  visible through the windows: a stout, slow man in his fifties, no fighter. Around midday he opens
  the hatch.
- The customers are among the poorer locals. Jorrith offers two dishes a day, one meal per person,
  and wants his bowls back; he knows his regulars and asks after their families. No customer shows
  any agent-like behaviour — no code-words, no dead drops — and many simply take food away,
  including children buying several meals for families. Caspian and Rell both note, from the
  Bridgemarch experience, that such customers are almost never agents.
- Caspian's Detect Magic at the hatch does not flare; only one or two customers carry faintly
  magical items, most likely priest-blessed charms. Looking through the hatch, the kitchen is small,
  tidy, and spartan, with nothing untoward visible.

## The Three Copper Hatch — lunch

Mags, Fabian and Trevick come to the hatch for lunch. Caspian shadows them invisibly, while Rell does
not take part — he maintains his hidden vantage throughout. Before rounding the corner, Mags gives
Trevick one of her small bones — claiming it as a keepsake — to plant on Jorrith so the party can
locate him later. From his vantage, Rell checks whether Fabian's conspicuous arrival flushes out any
lookouts; none scarper.

At the side door, while Mags makes small talk and orders meals, Trevick attempts the planting. His
first reach is clumsy, and Jorrith draws back, briefly suspicious he is being pickpocketed. Trevick
recovers with a charm offensive, leaning on a northern Travarian accent; Jorrith's manner softens —
possibly reminded of his wife — and the bone is placed. Jorrith warms noticeably, the most cheerful
he has been all morning.

Mags then drops the word "brood" into a sentence ("looking after a brood like this") and watches him
closely. There is no dramatic reaction, only a faint flicker, a small twitch, and a beat-too-long
look before his warmth fades to something guarded. Mags reads it as not fear but the weight of a
private burden — a man reminded of something that makes life difficult. The grounding is a single
high Insight read; it is a tell, not a confession.

Caspian, still invisible, drops cover beside Trevick afterward to quietly praise the recovery,
without telling the others he nearly blew it. The party eats — the "meat" dish proves to be little
more than a bone boiled for flavour — and disperses.

## The Pindle Brak — Mags, Fabian & Trevick

While Caspian and Rell keep the watch, the gossip group spends the afternoon at the Pindle Brak, a
gnome pub in the same quarter. The name means roughly "the cotter's assistant." It is built to gnome
scale, so Fabian and Trevick must stoop, and the regulars are mildly put out to have humans in one of
their few spaces.

- Fabian surprises the party by speaking fluent Gnomish, learned from his home mentor — a fact Mags
  did not know despite their long acquaintance. With gnomish songs and tales of his mentor he wins
  the regulars round, Mags helping unusually rather than letting him flounder.
- The pub serves three drinks: beer, an herbal spirit called the East Gate Sour, and an unnamed,
  potent spirit translating as "the bad idea," brewed each year for Shadows Meet and meant to be
  drunk on the festival night.
- Trevick downs "the bad idea." It burns, then brings on a swimming, underwater sense of altered
  perception; he relaxes into it and copes well, more amused than distressed.
- Fabian recalls (History) that such mild perception-altering brews are typical of Itharian rites,
  meant to put participants into a liminal, threshold state of awareness — a festival drink by
  design. He leads the pub in a song in Trevick's honour.

The gnomes' gossip yields little hard intelligence:

- They look forward to the festival; though not devout Itharians, they hold the gods are not
  exclusive and will join any festival worth joining. Some honour Shadows Meet simply by sitting in
  the pub drinking "the bad idea" all night.
- Temples to a god heard as "Braxen" (or "Braxton") — regarded as a genuine problem — are banned
  across Kaelindor, though not in Crownspire, so there is no overt worship of them. Other temples
  (Naelos, Calvyr) are commonplace. The gnomes know of no secret cults locally. The DM's "Braxen"
  is very likely Vraxyn, but this was not stated outright.
- On the Mirrowen's identity-mimics, those in from outlying villages firmly believe the danger is
  real — tales of meeting people on the road "who shouldn't have been there" — and describe a
  culture of vouching for anyone who must travel with strangers. They ham it up to scare Fabian.
- On dragons they know nothing new, only the common knowledge that the quarter sits on old
  dragon-landing ground; they can name no dragon expert, suggesting only that local historians must
  exist elsewhere.

Mags later sobers Trevick with Lesser Restoration, concentrating the comedown into a few seconds.
Trevick, drunk, hands a group of gnome patrons a vampire-romance novel — "You're So Vain" by Ivana
Moorhead — from a small stock he has been quietly reading on the road. Caspian arrives, finds Trevick
drunk during what he regards as work, and leaves in irritation.

## The hatch interior — Theodore

Caspian sends his familiar Theodore into the house through the hatch and watches through its eyes.

- Downstairs: two rooms and a privy — a kitchen onto the hatch, and a small living area — clean,
  tidy, and poor.
- Upstairs (a single half-width room under a sloping roof): a bedroom with a double bed, a clothes
  chest, and a heavy wooden cupboard. A framed drawing of a young, beautiful woman sits by the bed.
  Caspian, who was not part of the gossip, does not know she is Meera.
- There are no other doors, no hidden passages, and none of the symbols, blood, or scratchings the
  party found at the Bridgemarch house. Theodore is too small to open the chest or cupboard.

Caspian's outside survey maps the building: joined to its neighbour on one side, a narrow alley on
the other, a back door onto a small yard with a laundry line, a closed back window, and an upstairs
window. A further attempt to feel for a masking field — the absence of detection rather than the
presence of magic — is again inconclusive. Rell intends to wait for nightfall before moving directly
against the house.

## The plan — impersonating Soren

The party reconvenes and settles its approach. Rather than meet Jorrith as themselves, Rell will
impersonate Soren Dragan, the Brood agent whose dispatch the party intercepted in Bridgemarch, and
collect the orders Soren was meant to receive. The reasoning:

- The real Soren never received his instructions (the party took them) and so will not appear to
  expose the ruse.
- The intercepted dispatch named no codeword. The "rumour" Soren was sent to chase is already known
  to the party, from the document recovered at the original Bridgemarch house: a village called
  Kethon's Fold.
- Rell is the best suited — best liar, best at disguise — and a three-foot member would immediately
  betray the impersonation. Rell notes "Soren" is more typically a female name, and opts for an
  androgynous disguise.
- Caspian suggests Rell hold Detect Thoughts during the meeting, on the theory that any expected
  codeword or counter-sign would surface in Jorrith's foremost thoughts; Rell agrees it is reasonable
  but warns that digging deeper would be noticed.

Rell links the party with a long-lasting telepathic relay connecting up to three people to him at
once, reaching about a mile and lasting roughly an hour. It is not a group chat: each linked person
speaks only with Rell. He explains he honed and created it as an original spell on the road to
Greywatch — a framing consistent with the wizard identity he maintains, since the ability is in fact
his innate sorcery rather than a studied creation. The others take hidden positions to cover the
exits — Mags disguises herself, Fabian dons
an adventurer's costume, Caspian goes invisible, Trevick relies on stealth. Masks are set aside as
too conspicuous before the festival.

## Inside the hatch — Rell & Jorrith

Rell casts Disguise Self for an androgynous, "elfin" human look and layers it over a physical
adventurer's costume, then approaches the now-quiet hatch in the late afternoon.

He greets Jorrith by name and, performing coin tricks, uses Prestidigitation to flash the Furnace
Brood sigil on a coin, then states he is here pursuing a rumour. Jorrith does not miss the sign: he
loses colour, stares hard, and looks away. Rell casts Detect Thoughts and finds Jorrith a blank, as
if mind-shielded. Jorrith is plainly waiting for something — a counter-sign Rell does not have. Rell
improvises, drawing out the amulet as "the other bit" of the sign and claiming he could never
remember the verbal codes. Jorrith panics at the amulet being shown openly, gestures for it to be put
away, and ushers Rell in through the side door. Caspian, judging himself not brave enough to follow,
sends Theodore in to watch.

Inside, Jorrith sits heavily, as if his legs are giving out, head in his hands. He delivers the orders
he was holding, verbatim in substance:

- Travel about forty miles south to a village called Kethon's Fold, destroyed some ten weeks ago
  (around the beginning or middle of Ashend) when the ground gave way — a landslip or sinkhole
  swallowed it. Such collapses happen in the Mirrowen; the land is unstable.
- There are stories of caves, passages, or holes by which one can get underground into the buried
  village, and stories of people heard singing or crying somewhere below.
- Go in as deep as is safe, find anything strange that "does not belong" to such a village — treasure
  or anything anomalous — and bring it back to Jorrith to report, so the Brood can judge whether to
  send more people. Jorrith will pass on whatever is brought. That is the whole of the message.

Rell plays a weary fellow-agent and tells Jorrith he need not be so severe with him, being only an
adventurer. Reading the line, Jorrith's terror is plain, but a light of sympathy enters his eyes and
he asks, "Who did they take from you?" — implying the Brood coerce their agents by holding loved
ones hostage. Rell deflects, saying it is best not to discuss it. Jorrith apologises and admits this
is the first time he has actually had to do this. He confirms "Soren" is to return to him afterward,
and that he passes things on; that is all he knows. He clearly wants Rell gone, wants no deeper
involvement, and wants his name mentioned to no one.

Rell leaves, saying he will gather cave-diving supplies (plenty of festival merchants are in town)
and set off soon. Through Theodore, Caspian watches Jorrith close up the hatch, tidy obsessively to
steady his nerves, and then go upstairs and lie on the bed without sleeping, as if in the grip of a
panic attack. He opens no cupboard or chest. Theodore slips out.

## Decision and next steps

The party debates what to do with Jorrith and agrees not to turn him in to the authorities. He seems
a coerced, basically decent man trapped in the Brood's service, and they gain nothing by handing him
over — whereas keeping him in place preserves a live link to the Brood. Fabian's stated priority is
to take one of these agents alive this time, in contrast to Bridgemarch, to question them. Rell infers
a hostage is involved and that burning Jorrith as an asset would doom whomever the Brood hold; Fabian
guesses the wife is "almost certainly already dead," but Caspian and Mags note this is not known. (The
gossip has Meera dying of illness years ago; whether Jorrith has any living hostage is left open, and
the two are not assumed connected.)

The plan is to reach Kethon's Fold, which needs a local guide given the unstable country. There is an
official guild of guides that keeps records of who goes where; to stay off that record the party
prefers an unofficial, smuggler-type guide, which Rell can find through their lodging's contacts and
thieves'-cant markings. They will gather cave-diving supplies and leave before Shadows Meet, which is
a couple of days off and which they will therefore miss. The DM notes the festival's timing is
coincidental, not a plot hook, and confirms the next session will begin at the party's departure from
Greywatch or its arrival at Kethon's Fold.
