# Session 28 - 23/06/2026

*A narrated time-skip and catch-up session, covering the five-week journey from Bridgemarch to Greywatch. No combat.*

22 Threadbind, 1568

## Bridgemarch — departure

The party spends a few final days in Bridgemarch closing inquiries into Torston and making its report to the Chancellor before leaving on 22 Threadbind. Over a parting luncheon, the party catches up with Benny, who is thriving in his new role and training with the Queen's Guard, with a good chance of joining their number in time.

Pressed gently by Fabian, Benny reveals — nervous of how the party will react — that he was the one who reported their suspicions about Torston to the authorities. After the affair, Torston tried to draw Benny in more closely; Benny refused to side with him, and that refusal is how Torston's conspiring came to official notice. The disclosure has earned Benny considerable official favour and trust. The party reassures him. They privately resolve to stay discreet around Benny about their own ongoing work, reasoning that his moral objection to Torston's spying might not distinguish it from what they are now doing.

## The road — Bridgemarch to the Mirrowen

The journey runs about five weeks. The route goes north out of Bridgemarch, staying inside Trevaryn and hugging the Offendale–Trevaryn border, then east until it meets the Drake's Road, which runs south from Drake Mount into Greywatch. The terrain is rough, with no reliable accommodation; the party travels on foot. Most of the time is spent levelling and practising skills.

Two character threads advance on the road:

- Caspian and Fabian talk privately about returning for the silver-dragon bones now that Dakir, who disapproved of the scheme, has left. They agree to reconstruct their route from notes and consider enlisting Rell, and reason that extracting the bones could be framed as doing the forest a favour by removing what would otherwise draw unwanted traffic. This remains intention only; they note none of them can do the heavy lifting, and that help would be needed.
- Fabian conducts in-character interviews of the party for a planned written account, drawing out reflections from Caspian (on becoming more cautious and transparent; his interest in the dragon bones as a "puzzle") and Mags (on her time spent transformed into a tree, which she partly remembers, and her guardedness about the centaurs, whom she declines to discuss further by prior promise).

## Trevick rejoins

23 Dusklin

A few days outside Greywatch, Trevick rejoins the party. Travelling alone with Muriel and used to the wilds, he has moved much faster than the group despite leaving Kaelindor later, and has kept in contact via the paired sending-tokens Dakir made. He relays that Dakir has gone to learn more about the dragons.

The party brings Trevick up to date on events since he was last with them (in the Branweald). The recap establishes the following:

- Kroll had been killed under mysterious circumstances after shaking down the town; investigation revealed he was an agent of Korath, with the Furnace Brood — only him, not his men — and was heading into the forest under their orders.
- The party traced his orders to a cell in Bridgemarch, raided it with the town guard, and found a blood/ash shrine and, eventually, the Brood's orders. Those who received the orders killed themselves before they could be questioned.
- The recovered letters named three destinations; the party chose the Greywatch lead. They hold an address there.
- An adventurer, Soren Dragan, was being dispatched (or was about to be) to meet a contact in Greywatch and pursue a rumour. That is the lead being followed.
- The party now works for Chancellor Wicklow on a more permanent basis, retained to hunt the cultists.
- A map with marked places was found in Kroll's tent; Wicklow took it once shown, but Mags had already copied it. One marked place is plausibly near Trevick's hometown, though the DM notes it is a very large-scale map.
- Serris Dane, the boy who should have been Thayne, has been raised with the mayor of Greymere and will receive proper training; the villagers' money was recovered from the mercenaries.

On the gate, Rell alone advances the theory that the tamperer was a unicorn; the rest of the party does not share it, and Mags openly questions why they believe it. Rell's basis is an account given by a centaur during the forest negotiations: that an exceedingly rare unicorn had been seen in the forest in recent memory, regarded as exceptionally unusual. Rell connects dots the others do not — unicorns are fey-native, capable of complex magic, and have no place in the material forest, yet one has appeared seemingly from nowhere in an area that has just seen a crossing from the Feywild. Separately, and as news to Fabian and Mags, it comes out that the founding members — Caspian, Dakir, and Trevick — had once met two unicorns, Stargleam and Silverlily, on the Feywild Garden adventure (S1.04); these are distinct from the unicorn behind Rell's theory. The party also recalls, via Kroll's interrogated body, that the Furnace Brood were not responsible for the gate but were deeply interested in it, and that Kroll's mission was to check whether any trace of crossing had been left behind.

### Torston's fate, clarified

The catch-up resolves several points about Torston:

- Benny reported the party's suspicions, which brought Torston's conspiring to official attention.
- Torston was detained for a time, and his evidence was confiscated rather than withheld by choice.
- He was then reassigned to an "honourable" appointment in Crownspire. The party had checked, via Mags's Sending, that the reassignment was not a euphemism for death; he is alive and genuinely in Crownspire. The party reads the appointment as a means of removing him from the town.
- The party notes he asked them to continue the investigation but provided no evidence, leaving them effectively at square one.

### The Queen's Guard near Greymere

Trevick reports that, as he left Greymere, Queen's Guard officers were setting up nearby. The DM confirms they were relatively senior, made no attempt to conceal themselves, and that town gossip held they were arranging to station a much larger contingent of guardsmen close to Greymere. The party also raises a determination that the Queen's Guard — also called the Knights of the Silent Road — might have a loose association with the Itharis worshippers thought to have killed Kroll, framed as a senior-clergy assassination. This is left as a party theory.

The conversation also touches Wicklow: as a youth he was part of the Itharian orders, which feeds the rumours that he is a secret Itharian priest, rumours he denies vehemently. The party openly regards it as overwhelmingly likely that Wicklow had Kroll killed, on the grounds of Kroll's Itharian-style murder and Wicklow's Itharian ties.

## Briefing — the Mirrowen and Greywatch

The party makes history checks, with Fabian (taught about the region as part of his League education) and Trevick (from neighbouring Trevaryn) best-informed. Caspian and Rell recall only vague, half-remembered stories.

The party learns the following about the Mirrowen, colloquially the Tangles:

- It is not really a country, though technically counted among the League's and holding a Council seat — an area too magically damaged to belong cleanly to anyone, peeled off into its own jurisdiction.
- Its cause is unknown but pre-historical; consensus holds it was not always this way. Reality there is fragmented, with planes that would never normally touch butting against one another. Planar portals appear spontaneously or stay briefly stable; more often reality is simply twisted together untidily.
- The land lacks reliable geography — parts shift, and whole villages can disappear. It is borderline habitable, most disrupted in the centre and south; the south is effectively impassable, so traffic moves north toward Trevaryn.
- It is psychically dangerous, with a high incidence of people losing their minds, and full of strange, aberration-like creatures. Scholars disagree on whether these are mutations from planes in contact or something more fundamentally alien. Fabian's sister Valeria, an aberration-scholar, is noted as obsessed with such specimens and the museum debate around them.
- The land throws up echoes and mimicry of things it has touched — distinct from changelings or doppelgangers as races. Outside Greywatch there is a strong cultural emphasis on vouching for identity, since anyone who goes off alone is suspect on return.
- It has a very high incidence of innate magical gifts; many sorcerers are born there, and some come hoping to "quicken" their magic at rare, dangerous sites. It also holds valuable magical plants and occasionally-uncovered ancient sites, drawing treasure-seekers.
- It is held sacred by the Itharians as a place of thresholds and crossings, and draws Itharian pilgrimage.

On the politics:

- The Mirrowen is effectively a client of Trevaryn: its supplies come from the north, no ruler is appointed there without the approval of the council at Drake Mount, and Crownspire prefers Drake Mount control the region rather than Mooncross in Kalredor.
- Trevaryn is a steadfast League member on good terms with Crownspire, supplying soldiers and garrisoning the eastern passes of the Stonemarch against the Elenfain. Kalredor, to the south, is viewed with more suspicion, having been the kingdom most influenced by the elves before the war.

On Greywatch, also called the Beacon:

- It is the only substantial town in the Mirrowen, walled and heavily warded to hold a pocket of stable land. It is a fortified garrison town more than a civilian one, with watched gates and formal recording of arrivals.
- It was created as a watch-post and warning beacon for anything emerging from the Mirrowen toward Trevaryn; its central keep-tower bears a beacon that burns different-coloured flames, watched from stations in Trevaryn. It also serves as a market for goods brought out of the region.
- Its walls are an irregular shape because they were built around a dragon landing-ground, now filled with houses — a relic of Trevaryn's old closeness to the dragons, mirrored at Drake Mount.

The party also learns of the Itharian hierarchy that overt priests are the lowest rank and senior priests do not announce themselves, and that the Itharians regard the Horizonborn with reverence; meeting Rell on the festival eve would be taken as a good omen. The festival, Shadows Meet (also the Vigil), falls on 1 Ithar's Veil, runs from dusk through the night, and involves masks; it is one of the few non-secret Itharian rites, and non-Itharians do not normally participate.

## Arrival at Greywatch

28 Dusklin

The party descends from the heights of Trevaryn toward Greywatch, which sits on a patch of cleared land; the terrain beyond looks irregular and not naturally formed. The town is overcrowded ahead of the festival, with pilgrims sleeping in haylofts and camping uneasily outside the warded walls despite guardsmen moving them on.

The party debates how to arrive. Concerned about a possible mole in Wicklow's court, they decide against using Fabian's family name (his brother Marcellus shares the recognisability) or throwing his weight around for lodging. They settle on a low-profile cover story as treasure-seekers — a common, unremarkable reason to come here — with Caspian presented as a prospecting wizard and the others as his servants; they agree not to lead with hunting an ancient of Korath. A party call-sign is discussed against the region's mimicry but treated as a precaution only.

At the gate-checkpoint, where arrivals are recorded but not interrogated, the party gives false names and states "adventure" as its purpose. They buy festival masks on the approach. Rell then follows thieves'-cant markings to discreet, less-reputable lodging, judged beneath some of the party's tastes but appropriately low-profile. Trevick, who had intended to camp outside, is persuaded to stay within the walls.

The session ends with the party settled in Greywatch, the contact's address in hand, a few days before Shadows Meet.
